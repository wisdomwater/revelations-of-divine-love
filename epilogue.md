## Epilogue

Every journey eventually reaches its final page.

Yet the truest journeys never really end.

Julian's visions lasted only a short time. The words you have spent these days reading were born from a handful of extraordinary hours. But she lived with those hours for the rest of her life. She returned to them in prayer, in silence, in suffering, and in joy. She allowed them to shape not merely what she believed about God, but how she saw the whole world.

That is the invitation now placed before you. You have not simply finished a book. You have been invited to practice a different way of seeing.

The world around you has not changed. There will still be mornings when anxiety greets you before peace does. There will still be disappointments that leave you wondering what God is doing. You will still discover places within yourself that need healing. You will still fail in ways that surprise you.

Julian would not ask you to deny any of this. She would simply ask you not to let these things become the deepest truth about your life.

The deepest truth is that you are loved. Before your first breath, you were loved. In your greatest faithfulness, you are loved. In your greatest weakness, you are loved. When your prayers feel full of light, you are loved. When they feel empty and distracted, you are loved. Nothing in this book makes sense apart from that single reality. Nor does the Christian life.

If there is one practice I hope you carry with you, it is this:

Whenever fear becomes louder than love, pause.

Whenever shame becomes more convincing than mercy, pause.

Whenever you find yourself imagining a God who has grown weary of you, pause.

Then remember Julian's vision. Remember Christ seated quietly within the soul.

Remember His words:

*You shall not be overcome.*

Remember His promise:

*All shall be well.*

Remember His final interpretation of every vision:

*Love was His meaning.*

Let these become more than beautiful quotations. Let them become the lens through which you read your own life.

As you continue your journey, you will undoubtedly encounter questions Julian never answered. There are mysteries that remain mysteries. There are passages of Scripture sincere Christians continue to understand differently. There are seasons when God will seem wonderfully near, and others when He will seem hidden.

Do not be afraid of that.

Julian teaches us that certainty is not the remedy to mystery. Love is.

A child does not understand everything about a loving parent. Yet the child rests. Perhaps that is the deepest wisdom Julian leaves us. Not that every question receives an answer. But that every question can be carried safely within the love of God.

So go back into your ordinary life. Wash the dishes. Answer your emails. Drive to work. Laugh with friends. Sit beside someone who is struggling.

Forgive.

Begin again after you fail. Pray when words come easily. Sit quietly when they do not.

And through it all, keep returning to the same simple center.

Love.

For in the end, love is not merely one virtue among many. It is the life of God. It is the shape of Christ. It is the work of the Spirit. It is the beginning from which you came. It is the path on which you now walk. And it is the home toward which you have always been traveling.

May the peace of Christ keep you.

May His light guide you.

May His love become both your journey and your destination.

Amen.
