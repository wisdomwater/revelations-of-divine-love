# Revelations of Divine Love

## About This Project

*Revelations of Divine Love* is the earliest surviving book in English written by a woman and one of the enduring treasures of Christian spirituality. Written by **Julian of Norwich** in the late fourteenth century, it records a series of visions centered on the inexhaustible love of God.

While the original remains remarkably beautiful, its Middle English vocabulary, medieval sentence structure, and historical context can make it difficult for many modern readers.

This project seeks to bridge that gap.

Rather than producing a literal translation, this repository contains a **devotional adaptation** that preserves Julian's theological vision while expressing it in contemporary English.

The goal is to allow today's readers to experience Julian's wisdom as though she were speaking directly to them.

---

## Editorial Philosophy

Each devotional seeks to remain faithful to Julian's meaning while emphasizing clarity, beauty, and spiritual formation.

The adaptation follows several guiding principles:

* Preserve Julian's theology.
* Modernize the language without modernizing the message.
* Write in Julian's voice rather than about Julian.
* Remove unnecessary historical barriers.
* Emphasize contemplation rather than explanation.
* Allow mystery to remain mystery.
* Encourage prayer more than analysis.

This is intentionally **not** a commentary or academic edition.

---

## Format

The adaptation consists of **86 daily readings**.

Each entry contains:

* A title
* A short contemplative aphorism
* Approximately 500–700 words of devotional reflection
* A brief Breath Prayer

The goal is to create a reading that takes roughly five minutes and naturally leads into silence and prayer.

---

## Themes

Throughout the devotional several themes continually emerge:

* God's love is the foundation of all things.
* Christ reveals the true heart of God.
* We are never separated from divine love.
* Trust often precedes understanding.
* Sin is real but never has the final word.
* Hope is stronger than despair.
* God is nearer than we imagine.
* "All shall be well."
* "Love was His meaning."

---

## Source Text

This adaptation is based primarily upon the public-domain text of **Julian of Norwich's *Revelations of Divine Love***.

No attempt has been made to modernize Julian's theology—only her language and presentation.

Readers are strongly encouraged to read the original alongside this adaptation.

## A Personal Note

I first came to Julian while searching for a deeper understanding of God's love.

Her words became a healing balm.

In a world where many Christians quietly carry fear, shame, anxiety, or the feeling that they never quite measure up, Julian gently reminds us that beneath every mystery lies the steadfast goodness of God.

If this adaptation helps even one person discover that same peace, then this work has fulfilled its purpose.

> "Love was His meaning."
