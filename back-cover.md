What if the deepest truth about God is not fear—but love?

More than six hundred years ago, during one of the darkest periods in English history, a woman named Julian of Norwich received a series of profound revelations that would become one of the greatest spiritual classics ever written. In a world marked by plague, uncertainty, and religious fear, Julian encountered a vision of God unlike anything she had expected: a God whose love never fails, whose mercy never runs dry, and whose presence is nearer than our own breath.

*Revelations of Divine Love* has comforted Christians for centuries. Yet its medieval language can feel distant to modern readers.

This devotional adaptation invites you to experience Julian's timeless wisdom in fresh, contemporary language through **86 daily readings** designed for prayer, reflection, and contemplation. Each meditation preserves the heart of Julian's message while speaking directly to the struggles of modern life—anxiety, shame, uncertainty, suffering, hope, and the longing to know God more deeply.

As you journey through these pages, you will discover a faith rooted not in striving but in trust, not in condemnation but in mercy, and not in fear but in the steadfast love of Christ. Along the way you will encounter some of Julian's most treasured insights:

* God is nearer than we imagine.
* We are never separated from His love.
* In our falling and in our rising, we are kept by one Love.
* "All shall be well."
* "Love was His meaning."

Whether you are discovering Julian for the first time or returning to a beloved spiritual companion, this book offers a gentle invitation to slow down, pray deeply, and allow the goodness of God to quiet your heart.

Come, sit beside one of Christianity's wisest voices, and learn again to rest in the love that has always been holding you.
