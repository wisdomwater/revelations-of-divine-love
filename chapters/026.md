## Day 26 – It Is I

> *Every true longing of the heart finds its home in Christ.*

After these things, the Lord revealed Himself to me with a glory beyond anything I had yet seen. It was not a glory that overwhelmed with distance, but one that drew me near with unspeakable tenderness. I understood that the soul is restless because it was made for Him. Every search, every unanswered longing, every quiet ache beneath the surface of life is, in its deepest truth, a longing for the One who is fullness itself. Until the soul rests in Him, it will continue to seek, even when it does not know what it is seeking.

Again and again the Lord spoke these simple words: "It is I." He did not hurry. He did not argue. He simply revealed Himself. "It is I whom you love. It is I whom you seek. It is I whom you desire. It is I whom you serve. It is I who am all." With every repetition, another veil seemed to fall away. I began to see that beneath every holy desire, every longing for truth, every search for beauty, every hunger for goodness, every hope for lasting joy, it is Christ who quietly waits to be found. We often give many names to the desires of our hearts, but at their deepest root they all belong to Him.

This does not mean that every desire is already pure or every path already clear. We become confused. We mistake lesser things for the fulfillment we seek. We ask created gifts to bear the weight of our infinite longing. Yet Christ does not grow impatient with our wandering. Again and again He gently speaks into the noise of our lives: "It is I." He calls us beneath our distractions, beneath our fears, beneath even our religious striving, until we recognize the familiar voice that has been drawing us all along. The One we have been searching for has never stopped searching for us.

Carry these words with you today. When your heart is restless, hear Him say, "It is I." When you feel alone, hear Him say, "It is I." When joy surprises you, when beauty catches your breath, when love awakens gratitude within you, hear Him say, "It is I." Let every good gift become a window rather than a destination. Let every longing become a path that leads you back to the One who is already nearer than your own soul. For Christ is not merely the answer to your deepest questions. He is the fulfillment of your deepest desire, and He never grows weary of reminding you: "It is I."

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *It is You.*

> Exhale: *My heart rests in You.*
