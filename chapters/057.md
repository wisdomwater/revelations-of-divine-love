## Day 57 – United in Christ

> *What God has joined to Himself, He will never let go.*

I was shown that in your deepest being you already belong to God. In that hidden place where your life first springs forth, you remain whole, noble, and beloved. There your soul never ceases to delight in the will of God, for it was fashioned from His goodness and for His joy. Yet I also saw that another part of your life knows weakness. Here you become weary. Here you forget. Here you stumble and feel divided within yourself. Do not think that this lower weakness is the whole truth of who you are. It is only the part that is still being healed by mercy.

God has not abandoned this fragile part of you. His mercy flows continually from His own goodness, restoring what has grown faint and filling what has been emptied. Grace is not merely forgiveness after failure. It is the patient life of God moving through every corner of your soul, quietly bringing together what has become scattered. Nothing that belongs to your true nature is lost. The One who created you is also the One who faithfully completes His work within you.

The greatest wonder I was shown is this: in Christ, our two natures have been united. The higher life that has always rested in God and the lower life that knows suffering have met perfectly in Him. The Son of God took upon Himself everything that is fragile in our humanity without losing the fullness of His divinity. He entered our weakness so that our weakness might be drawn into His strength. He entered our mortality so that our life might share in His own. From the beginning, this was the purpose of divine love. God made humanity because He loved us, and for that same love He chose to become one of us.

Everything that leads you toward life flows from this union. Faith awakens because Christ has already joined Himself to you. Hope grows because He carries your future within His own life. Love deepens because His Spirit quietly renews the goodness already planted within your soul. Every grace, every virtue, every holy desire is not something added from outside, but something nourished by the life of Christ dwelling within you. He has gathered every gift into Himself so that, in time, He may unfold them in you.

I was also shown a tenderness that is difficult to put into words. As surely as Mary became the mother of the Saviour, so she became the mother of all who are gathered into Him. And Christ Himself is our true Mother, in whom we are continually born into new life. We do not come to Him once and then depart. We remain within Him always. His love is a place of unending birth, where the soul is continually nourished, strengthened, and formed into His likeness. You have never outgrown your need to be held there, and He has never grown weary of holding you.

So let your heart rest in this mystery. Christ does not merely walk beside you or wait for you at the end of the journey. He lives within you, and you live within Him. He delights to reign quietly in your understanding, to rest peacefully within your soul, and to shape your life from the inside outward. Offer Him your attention. Learn His ways. Trust His patient work. For your truest life has always been hidden in Him, and what He has united to Himself shall remain united forever.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *You live in me.*

> **Exhale:** *I live in You.*
