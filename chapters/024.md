## Day 24 – Look How I Loved You

> *The open heart of Christ is your everlasting home.*

Then Christ turned His gaze toward the wound in His side, not with sorrow, but with joy. As He looked upon it, He invited me to look as well. I was drawn through that wounded place and discovered not darkness, but a spacious dwelling filled with peace. His opened side was not merely the mark of suffering. It had become a place of welcome, wide enough to receive every soul that longs for Him. There I understood that the deepest refuge has never been found in escaping life, but in entering more fully into the heart of divine love.

I saw the precious blood and water that had been poured out without reserve, and I beheld His heart opened completely. Nothing was hidden. Nothing was held back. The love of God is not cautious or guarded. It does not measure what it can safely give. It gives itself entirely. As I gazed upon this mystery, my thoughts were lifted beyond the wounds to the eternal Love from which they flowed—the Love that has always been, that is now, and that shall never cease. Before there was a cross, there was love. The cross simply unveiled what had always filled the heart of God.

Then the Lord spoke words that have remained with me ever since: "Look how I loved you." He did not say them to awaken guilt or to remind me of what I owed Him. He spoke them to awaken joy. It was as though He said, "My beloved, see how deeply you have always been held. See how gladly I have sought you. See how your salvation delights My heart." His suffering had already become joy because love had accomplished its purpose. What He desired most was not to dwell upon the pain He had endured, but to share with me the happiness that His love had secured.

Whenever you begin to doubt your place with God, return to this vision. Listen again to those gentle words: "Look how I loved you." Do not hear them as a demand to prove yourself worthy. Hear them as an invitation to rest. The heart of Christ stands open still, making room for you with unending tenderness. His greatest delight is not that you fear Him, but that you rejoice with Him. Enter that spacious love today. Make your home there, and let your life be quietly transformed by the certainty that the One who loved you from the beginning will never cease to do so.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You have loved me.*

> Exhale: *I rest in Your heart.*
