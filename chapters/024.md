## Day 23 – The Glad Giver

> *Christ does not give with reluctance, but with joy.*

I was shown that the Passion of Christ, though full of sorrow, is also filled with joy beyond our understanding. This is a deep mystery. The suffering was real, and no heart should look upon it lightly. Yet beneath the suffering was the gladness of divine love accomplishing what it most desired: our healing, our rescue, our return. The Father is pleased. The Son is glorified. The Holy Spirit is satisfied. All the life of God rejoices in the saving work of love.

Christ showed me His Passion in many ways: the bleeding, the fading of His face, the wounded body, the long dying. These revealed the cost of love. But then He showed me something more hidden: the joy within love. He does not wish your soul to remain only in sorrow over what He suffered. He wants you to receive the comfort and strength that flow from His gladness. For you are His joy. In you He delights without end, and by His grace you are invited to delight in Him.

Think of a generous giver who cares less about the cost of the gift than about the joy it brings to the beloved. If the gift is received with wonder and gratitude, the giver does not count the labor as loss. The joy of pleasing the beloved outweighs everything. So it is with Christ. He asks nothing from you as payment for His travail. He asks only that you receive His love gladly, that you allow His gift to comfort you, and that your heart be opened to the joy He already has in your salvation.

So do not receive grace as though it were handed to you with weariness or regret. Receive it as a gift offered by One whose whole desire is to gladden and heal you. Let thanksgiving rise gently within you, not from obligation, but from wonder. The love that saved you is not strained. It is abundant, free, and glad. Carry this into your day: you are not a burden Christ regrets bearing. You are the beloved He rejoices to bring home.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You give with joy.*
>
> Exhale: *I receive with thanks.*
