## Day 33 – Content with What Is Given

> *The deepest peace is found not in knowing everything, but in trusting the One who does.*

There have been times when I longed for God to answer every question that stirred within me. I wanted to see what lay beyond the veil. I wished to understand every mystery of judgment, every hidden purpose, every unseen corner of eternity. My desire did not spring from curiosity alone, but from a longing to know God more fully and to live more faithfully. Yet the Lord did not grant every answer I sought. Instead, He gave me something greater: the grace to trust Him even where my understanding could not follow.

I have learned that God does not reveal everything because love does not require complete explanation. He gives what nourishes the soul and withholds what would only burden it before its time. He has shown us His goodness. He has shown us His mercy. He has shown us His heart in Christ. These are not small gifts. They are the light by which every other mystery must be approached. When I found myself wanting to look beyond what He had chosen to reveal, I gently discovered that His silence was not absence. It was another expression of His wisdom.

Do not imagine that hidden things are signs of God's reluctance to be known. Rather, they remind you that the One you love is infinitely greater than your mind can contain. If every mystery could be explained, wonder would disappear, and trust would no longer be needed. There is a holy freedom in allowing God to remain God. You need not carry the weight of solving what He has chosen to keep within His own loving counsel. The heart grows lighter when it releases what it was never meant to hold.

So fix your attention upon what has already been made known. Give thanks for every glimpse of grace, every evidence of mercy, every moment in which Christ has revealed the Father's love. Leave the hidden work in God's faithful hands. The saints rejoice not because every mystery has been mastered, but because every mystery rests safely within perfect Love. Let your soul learn the same quiet contentment. Be equally grateful for what God reveals and for what He conceals, trusting that both arise from the same boundless goodness. In time, all that truly needs to be known will be given. Until then, let wonder become another form of worship.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Your wisdom is enough.*

> **Exhale:** *I rest in Your mystery.*
