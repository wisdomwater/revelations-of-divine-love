## Day 32 – The Hidden Work of God

> *What love has begun, love will one day bring to completion.*

There are moments when the world seems too broken to mend. You witness cruelty that cannot be undone, losses that cannot be replaced, and wounds that seem destined to remain forever open. Your heart asks how such things could ever lead to goodness. I have stood before that same mystery. Yet the Lord gently reminded me not to measure His promises by the limits of my understanding. He asked me instead to trust the boundless wisdom of divine love. What seems impossible to you is not impossible to God. Hold fast to this: all shall be well. Not because you can yet see how, but because Love has already seen the ending.

Do not think that God watches only the great movements of history while overlooking the quiet details of your life. When the Lord spoke of *all manner of things*, I understood that nothing is beneath His care. The smallest kindness, the hidden grief you carry, the unnoticed tears, the quiet prayers that never leave your lips—none of these are forgotten. Divine love is never distracted. The One who fashioned the stars also treasures the smallest movements of your heart. Nothing that belongs to love is ever lost.

Yet there are questions that remain beyond your sight. You long to know how every injustice will be answered, how every sorrow will be healed, how every promise of God will finally be fulfilled. I was shown that there is a great work already prepared within the heart of God, hidden from every created mind. We are not told how it will unfold or by what means it will come to pass. We are only invited to know that it exists. This hidden work has been held in the love of God from before the beginning of time, awaiting the moment when He will reveal the fullness of His goodness. Sometimes peace is found not by possessing the answer, but by trusting the One who does.

There are truths that may seem impossible to reconcile. Faith can leave you standing between what you have been taught and what your heart longs to believe about the goodness of God. Do not be afraid if you find yourself there. I found myself there as well. I was not given every explanation. Instead, I was given a promise: *What is impossible for you is not impossible for Me.* Those words became enough. They taught me that the limits of human understanding need not become the limits of divine mercy. One day you will see what you cannot yet imagine. Every promise God has spoken will prove true, and everything that now stands wounded or unfinished will be gathered into a goodness greater than you have ever dared to hope. Until that day, let your soul rest not in certainty about the hidden work, but in confidence in the One whose love is already accomplishing it.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Nothing is impossible for You.*

> Exhale: *I trust Your hidden work.*
