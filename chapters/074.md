## Day 74 – Reverence Without Fear

> *Perfect love does not drive you away from God; it draws you gently into His embrace.*

I have come to see that not every fear is the same. Some fears awaken us, while others imprison us. There are moments when fear startles us from carelessness, helping us recognize our need for God. There are sorrows that humble us and lead us to seek His mercy more earnestly. These fears can become servants of grace, for they stir the sleeping soul and remind it where true help is found. Yet they are not meant to become our dwelling place. They are only guides, leading us toward something greater than fear itself.

There is another kind of fear that quietly steals the heart's peace. It whispers that God may not be as good as you hoped. It tells you that your failures have placed His love beyond your reach or that His patience has finally been exhausted. This fear often disguises itself as humility, yet it bears bitter fruit. It turns your eyes inward until you see little except your own weakness. It keeps you measuring yourself instead of resting in the mercy of Christ. I was shown that this fear does not please our Lord, for it forgets the very goodness He delights to reveal. He does not desire that His children live in suspicion of His heart.

The fear that does delight Him is altogether different. It is the reverence born of love. Imagine a little child who, frightened by the world, runs without hesitation into the arms of a loving mother. The child's fear does not create distance; it deepens trust. So it is with the soul that truly knows God. Reverent fear does not make you hide from Him. It draws you nearer. It remembers His greatness while rejoicing in His tenderness. It knows that He is holy beyond imagining, yet also nearer than your own breath. Such reverence is gentle, joyful, and filled with quiet wonder. The more deeply love grows, the less this reverent fear feels like fear at all, until it becomes simple adoration.

Love and reverence belong together. Love without reverence may become careless, forgetting the majesty of the One who loves us. Reverence without love becomes cold and burdensome, forgetting the kindness of the One before whom we stand. But when they grow together, they become like two roots feeding the same living tree. Love gives you confidence to draw near. Reverence keeps your heart awake to the sacredness of the One you embrace. Together they lead you into a friendship with God that is both intimate and full of awe.

Therefore, ask your Lord for this holy gift. Pray that He would teach you to love Him with a humble heart, to trust Him with unwavering confidence, and to revere Him with joyful wonder. The more you trust Him, the more your life becomes an offering of praise. Whenever lesser fears begin to trouble you, let them become reminders to return to His embrace. Run to Him as a child runs home. There you will discover that His greatness is not something to flee from but the safest place your soul will ever know. For the God who is worthy of all reverence is the very God whose love has been drawing you to Himself from the beginning.

> **Inhale:** *Holy and loving God.*
