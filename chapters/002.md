## Day 2 – The Wounds That Heal

> *The deepest wounds are the ones that open us to love.*

There was a time when I believed I needed extraordinary experiences in order to draw nearer to God. I longed to stand beside those who witnessed Christ's suffering, to see with my own eyes what love was willing to endure. I even imagined that hardship itself might become a doorway into greater faith. Yet beneath every desire was something simpler than I first understood. I was not truly seeking visions, nor pain, nor signs. I was seeking a heart that could love as Christ loves.

As the years unfolded, I learned to place even my deepest longings into God's hands. It is no burden to tell the Lord what your heart desires, provided you are willing to add, "Only if it leads me closer to You." There is a quiet freedom in releasing even your holiest hopes. God is never offended by your longing, but love is perfected when trust becomes greater than the outcome you imagine. You do not need to force your own path toward God. The One who loves you knows the road far better than you do.

Yet there were three prayers I could never stop praying, for they reached beyond any particular circumstance. I asked for the wound of true sorrow over all that separates me from love, not so I would live beneath shame, but so my heart would remain tender and honest before God. I asked for the wound of compassion, that another person's suffering would never become someone else's concern while leaving my own heart untouched. And I asked for the wound of unending longing, that no earthly comfort would ever satisfy me more than the presence of the One who created me. These are wounds that do not diminish life. They make it more fully alive.

Perhaps you, too, carry prayers that have remained with you through every season. Do not be afraid of them if they draw you toward greater humility, deeper compassion, and a more steadfast love for God. The Spirit often works through the quiet desires that refuse to leave us. Hold them gently, trusting that the One who planted those longings within you is also tending their growth. In time, you may discover that what first felt like a wound has become the place where divine love enters most deeply.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my heart.*
>
> Exhale: *Fill it with Your love.*
