## Day 76 – Never Run from Love

> *When you fall, do not run from God; run more deeply into His embrace.*

I have learned that the soul which truly begins to see the beauty of Christ no longer fears punishment as much as it grieves anything that wounds love. Sin is not terrible because God delights in condemning it. Sin is terrible because it turns the heart, however briefly, away from the One who is Life itself. The more clearly you behold the goodness of your Lord, the more naturally you will desire to remain close to Him. Therefore, do not spend your days consumed with fear of hell. Let your deepest desire be to remain within the love of God. And when you discover that you have wandered, do not remain there. Rise quickly, for love is already calling you home.

I was also shown to be careful in the way I look upon the failures of others. It is easy for another person's sin to cloud the heart. We may become fascinated by it, disturbed by it, or quietly begin to judge it. When this happens, our vision of God grows dim, like a landscape hidden behind a heavy mist. If you must look upon another's weakness, let your gaze be filled with compassion rather than condemnation. Grieve with those who grieve. Pray for those who stumble. Remember that every soul is deeply loved by the same Lord who has shown you mercy. Compassion clears the eyes to see God again, while judgment darkens them.

The greatest wisdom I have been shown is wonderfully simple: remain close to Jesus in every circumstance. Whether your heart feels joyful or weary, whether you feel faithful or ashamed, whether you believe yourself strong or painfully weak, do not withdraw from Him. He does not measure His love according to the changing condition of your soul. His love remains steadfast through every season. We are the ones who change. We are the ones who wander. Yet His invitation is always the same: stay near Me. There is no place where His mercy cannot reach you if you are willing to receive it.

Be watchful, then, for the quiet voice that does not come from God. It speaks in the language of discouragement. It reminds you of every promise you have broken, every prayer you neglected, every opportunity you wasted. It whispers that because you have failed before, you will surely fail again. At first this voice may sound honest, even humble, but its purpose is always the same: to persuade you to keep your distance from the One who loves you. It hopes that shame will accomplish what temptation could not. If it can make you believe you are unwelcome, it has hidden the face of your truest Friend.

Do not listen to that lie. Whenever shame tells you to turn away, turn instead toward Christ. He has never asked you to earn your place beside Him. He asks only that you remain with Him. Let His beautiful countenance become stronger in your memory than every accusing thought. Let His mercy become more familiar than your failures. For your everlasting Friend has not grown weary of you. He is waiting with the same patient love that has carried you from the beginning, and His greatest desire is not that you should hide your weakness, but that you should bring it into the healing light of His presence.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Keep me near You.*

> **Exhale:** *I will not run from Love.*
