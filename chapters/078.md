## Day 78 – The Light That Reveals and Heals

> *God never reveals your weakness to shame you, only to draw you more deeply into His love.*

Our gracious Lord does not show you your weakness to discourage you. He shows it because He loves you. If He were to reveal the full weight of your brokenness apart from His mercy, your heart could not bear it. So He shines His gentle light upon your soul only as much as love knows you are ready to receive. His grace always comes before His revealing. His mercy surrounds every truth He asks you to face. He is like a careful physician who uncovers a wound only because He already intends to heal it. Never imagine that God's purpose in exposing your weakness is condemnation. His purpose is always restoration.

I was shown four things that every soul needs to remember. First, God is your beginning. Every breath, every moment of life, every good desire has its source in Him. Second, even when you wander, He never ceases to guard you with patient love. You often do not realize how much He protects you, even from dangers you cannot see. Third, He gently makes known the places where you have gone astray, not to accuse you, but to awaken you. And fourth, while you are changing, He never changes. His love remains steadfast. His heart toward you does not rise and fall with your faithfulness. He waits with unwavering patience until you willingly return and discover that He has been holding you all along.

When you begin to see your sins honestly, do not lose heart. Such sight is itself a gift of grace. Only love can make you truthful without leaving you in despair. There is a humility that grows from seeing yourself as you truly are, and it is a beautiful thing. It loosens the grip of pride and frees you from pretending to be what you are not. Yet true humility never ends with self-accusation. It always leads you back into the healing embrace of Christ. The same light that reveals your wounds also reveals the hands that are already reaching to bind them.

I was also shown that this journey belongs to us together. The one who has walked furthest with God still has reason to remain humble. The one who feels weakest may still take courage from those who have gone before. None of us stand above another in our need for grace. We are bound together by one mercy, one hope, and one love. As you grow in holiness, do not imagine that you have left behind the need for humility. The nearer you draw to God, the more clearly you will see both His greatness and your continual dependence upon Him. This is not a burden but a freedom, for humility makes room for joy.

Even the deepest moments of contemplation are not meant to lift you above your humanity. They are meant to draw you more deeply into it. If you are ever granted a glimpse of God's beauty, let it also make you gentler toward your own weakness and the weakness of others. You cannot discover this wisdom by your own effort, nor will your fears ever teach it to you. It is God Himself who lovingly opens your eyes at the proper time. Give thanks, then, whenever His light uncovers something that still needs healing. It is the sure sign that Love has not finished its beautiful work within you. The One who reveals is the One who restores, and He will never cease His patient labor until you are wholly one with Him.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Reveal me in love.*

> **Exhale:** *Heal me completely.*
