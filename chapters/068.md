## Day 68 – You Shall Not Be Overcome

> *The promise is not that life will spare you, but that love will keep you.*

The vision rested before me in perfect peace. I saw my Lord seated within the soul, unmoved and untroubled, and I understood that His resting was not the stillness of one who has withdrawn, but the confidence of One whose kingdom cannot be shaken. His place within you is everlasting. He does not come and go according to your feelings. He does not abandon His dwelling when fear arises or suffering enters your life. The more your heart learns to behold Him there, the more you begin to share in His own peace. Love quietly shapes you into the likeness of the One you behold.

Then the Lord spoke to me again—not with the sound of a human voice, but with a certainty that reached deeper than words. He gently assured me that what I had seen was no illusion. I was not deceived. I was not abandoned to my own imagination. He invited me to receive these revelations with trust, to treasure them, to find comfort in them, and to remain steadfast in the truth they revealed. His invitation was not only for me. It is for you as well. When God gives light for your journey, hold it close. There will be days when the path grows dim, but the truth He has planted in your heart remains faithful even when your sight grows weak.

Then came the words that have echoed through my soul ever since: **You shall not be overcome.** I have returned to them often, especially when life has grown heavy. Notice what our Lord did not say. He did not promise that you would never be troubled. He did not say you would never know grief, illness, disappointment, temptation, or loss. He never hid the reality of suffering. Instead, He offered something deeper than escape. He promised that none of these things would have the final victory over you. They may wound you for a time. They may weary your body or burden your heart. But they cannot separate you from the love that holds you, nor can they destroy the life Christ is bringing to completion within you.

This is why hope is stronger than optimism. Optimism depends upon circumstances becoming favorable. Hope rests upon the unchanging faithfulness of God. Even when everything around you seems uncertain, His promise remains firm. You may feel shaken, but you will not be abandoned. You may stumble, but you will not be forsaken. You may pass through valleys where the light is difficult to find, yet beneath every step His everlasting arms continue to carry you. The strength that preserves you has never depended upon your own ability to endure. It has always rested in the One whose love cannot fail.

So let these words become a quiet refuge whenever fear seeks to claim your heart. Carry them into seasons of joy and into seasons of sorrow. Repeat them when faith feels effortless, and repeat them again when faith feels like little more than a whispered prayer. Let them settle deep within you until they become steadier than every anxious thought. For the God who has revealed His heart to you is the same God who walks beside you still. His final word is not despair, nor fear, nor defeat. His final word is the same today as it has always been: **You shall not be overcome.** And because this is true, you may entrust your whole life to Him, knowing that, in the end, **all shall be well.**
