## Day 17 – The Sorrow Love Chooses

> *The deepest wound of love is not our own suffering, but the suffering of the One we love.*

As I gazed upon Christ, I saw not only the wounds upon His body but the love that held Him there. Every moment upon the cross revealed a heart that refused to turn away. His thirst, His weakness, His long endurance—none of these were signs that love had failed. They were the very shape love had taken. I was shown this not so that I would become occupied with pain itself, but so that I might behold the immeasurable steadfastness of the One whose love never retreats, even when it costs everything.

As I looked more deeply, my own heart began to ache in ways I had never known. At first I believed I desired to understand His suffering, but when I was allowed to glimpse even a small measure of it, I realized how little I had understood my own prayer. There are mysteries we think we are ready to receive until they are placed before us. Then we discover that love is deeper, sorrow is heavier, and compassion asks more of us than we imagined. Yet even this realization is a gift, for it teaches humility. We need not grasp every mystery. It is enough to remain near the One who bears it.

I came to understand that there is a sorrow unlike every other sorrow. It is not born of fear for ourselves, nor of despair, but of love. When someone you cherish suffers, your own heart cannot remain untouched. You would gladly carry their burden if you could. In this way I learned that the greatest pain was not my own but seeing Christ, who is my life and joy, endure such suffering out of love for the world. Love draws us so deeply into another's life that their grief becomes our own, not to crush us, but to enlarge our hearts.

If you ever find yourself grieving over the wounds of Christ, or over the suffering of another, do not be afraid of those tears. Love has opened your heart, and that opening is holy. But do not remain only in sorrow. Lift your eyes beyond the cross to the love that shines through it. Every wound speaks of mercy. Every act of endurance declares that you have never been abandoned. The One who thirsted for you still thirsts—not for your perfection, but for your willing heart. Draw near without fear, and let your love answer His with quiet trust, for where love is shared, sorrow is never the final word.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your love remains.*

> Exhale: *My heart remains with You.*
