## Day 16 – Love That Holds Nothing Back

> *Love does not turn away from suffering when suffering is the path to communion.*

I was shown the face of Christ as He drew near to death, and I could not look upon Him without both sorrow and wonder. The beauty that had shone with life slowly gave way to the pale stillness of One who had poured Himself out completely. I watched as strength faded, as every breath became more costly, and as His body yielded to the long work of suffering. Yet I was not shown these things so that I would despair. I was shown them so that I might understand how far divine love is willing to go to remain with us.

As I looked, I realized that love does not measure its cost before it gives itself. Christ did not withdraw when pain became severe, nor did He abandon the work of mercy when it demanded everything of Him. He remained faithful through every moment, not because suffering itself was good, but because His love was greater than the suffering He endured. Every wound declared the same quiet truth: "I will not leave you. I will stay with you to the very end."

When you pass through seasons of loss, remember this vision. God does not watch your pain from a safe distance. The One who formed you has entered the deepest places of human sorrow and has carried suffering within His own flesh. There is no loneliness you experience that He has not made His own. There is no grief so hidden that His compassion cannot find it. Even when your heart feels dry and exhausted, as though all strength has been spent, you are walking with One who knows that road from the inside.

Do not be afraid to look upon the cross with open eyes. Beneath its sorrow is a love that refuses to let death have the final word. The Passion reveals not only what humanity can inflict, but what divine love will endure rather than abandon the beloved. Hold this close whenever fear whispers that you have been forgotten. The face of Christ, marked by suffering yet radiant with unyielding love, is the everlasting promise that nothing—not pain, not loss, not even death itself—can separate you from the heart of God.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You stayed with me.*

> Exhale: *I remain in Your love.*
