## Day 28 – You Never Suffer Alone

> *Every act of true compassion is Christ loving through you.*

When I was shown the suffering of Christ, my heart was filled with compassion for Him. Then the Lord widened my heart until I felt compassion for all who belong to Him. I saw how His beloved people are often shaken by sorrow, misunderstanding, disappointment, and loss. There are seasons when life seems to strip away every certainty, leaving the soul exposed like a cloth caught in a strong wind. Yet even then, Christ does not look upon His children with disappointment. He beholds them with profound tenderness, already seeing the glory that His love is quietly preparing within them.

I understood that there are burdens we carry which are no fault of our own. There are wounds that come simply because we are learning to love in a world that has not yet been made whole. At times we are misunderstood, overlooked, or rejected. These things grieve the heart of Christ, yet He does not allow them to be empty sorrows. He gently uses even these painful places to loosen our grip on pride, on the need to be admired, and on the fragile identities we build for ourselves. What feels like loss today may become the very place where humility flowers and where our hearts learn the freedom of belonging to God alone.

Then I was shown a beautiful mystery. Every time you feel genuine compassion for another, Christ is loving through you. Whenever your heart aches for someone else's sorrow, whenever you forgive instead of retaliating, whenever you quietly carry another's burden, you are sharing in His own life. Compassion is not merely a human virtue. It is the living presence of Christ within the soul. His mercy continues to move through willing hearts, bringing comfort where there was loneliness and hope where there was despair.

So when suffering visits you, remember that you do not walk that road by yourself. Christ is not merely waiting for you at the end of your journey; He is beside you in every step of it. His own suffering has gone deeper than yours ever will, and because of that, your pain is already held within His redeeming love. Let this keep your heart from despair. Let it soften every temptation toward bitterness. And when you encounter another who is weary, offer them even the smallest kindness. You may think it is only your compassion, but I tell you truly: it is Christ, still reaching into the world through those who have made room for His love.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You suffer with me.*

> Exhale: *Love through me.*
