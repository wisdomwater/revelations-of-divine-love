## Day 52 – Mourning and Joy Together

> *The love that suffers with you is the love that will restore you.*

I saw that God rejoices to be everything your soul needs. He rejoices to be your Father, your Mother, your true Spouse, your Brother, and your Saviour. These are not distant names, nor cold titles, but living joys within God Himself. He delights to be near you in every way love can be near. He does not love you reluctantly. He does not save you with weariness. He rejoices to hold you, to guide you, to join Himself to you, and to bring you into His own blessed life.

Yet while you live in this world, you carry a strange mingling within you. You have Christ risen in you, and you have also the sorrow of Adam’s fall. In Christ you are kept, strengthened, and lifted into trust. In Adam you feel weakness, confusion, temptation, and pain. Sometimes the light is clear, and you know yourself held by grace. At other times the sweetness is hidden, and you feel only heaviness. Then you may wonder where you stand, or whether God is still near. But do not judge your soul only by what you feel in the dark.

When you cannot feel comfort, remember this: the will that turns toward God is itself His work within you. When you groan against what is false, when you long to be free from sin, when you desire God even without sweetness, grace is already alive in you. You may fall into blindness for a time, but Christ your Keeper does not fall away from you. He is with you in heaven, drawing you upward. He is with you on earth, leading your steps. He is within your soul, dwelling, ruling, and keeping you more deeply than you can understand.

So you have, for now, matter for mourning, because sin is the cause of Christ’s pain. Love does not ask you to call evil good or to pretend your wounds do not matter. But you have, more lastingly, matter for joy, because endless love made Him willing to suffer. His suffering was not forced from Him. It was the free movement of divine tenderness toward the beloved. Therefore hate only sin, not yourself. Grieve what harms love, but do not despair of Love. Let your sorrow be clean and humble, not hopeless.

When you fall, rise readily. Do not sink so low that you forget mercy, and do not grow so careless that you forget reverence. Acknowledge your weakness simply, without hiding and without terror. You cannot stand for even the blink of an eye except by grace, and this is not shameful. It is the truth that makes trust possible. Your part is to turn again toward God. God’s part is to excuse, heal, restore, and raise you higher than you can imagine.

For God sees you in a way you cannot yet fully see yourself. You see the fall; He sees also the restoration. You see the wound; He sees the glory His love will make from it. In the lower part of life, there is pain, mercy, forgiveness, and healing. But in the higher truth of God, there is one love, one joy, one endless union. Carry both mourning and joy gently within you today. Mourn what love must heal, and rejoice that Love has already begun.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Hold my sorrow.*

> Exhale: *Raise me in joy.*
