## Day 40 – Hate the Sin, Love the Soul

> *Love does not ignore sin; it refuses to let sin have the final word.*

One of the greatest kindnesses the Lord gives is that He does not leave you unaware of your sin. By the gentle light of His mercy, He helps you see what keeps you from the fullness of life. At first, you may imagine that this light reveals an angry God standing against you. But stay a little longer. As your heart turns toward Him in repentance, you will discover that He has never withdrawn His love. When you return, He does not greet you with coldness or reproach. He welcomes you with joy, as one who has waited patiently for a beloved child to come home. In all your wandering, He has remained near. In all your sorrow, He has remained faithful.

Do not mistake this mercy for permission to treat sin lightly. The more deeply you come to know the love of God, the less attractive sin becomes. Fear may restrain your actions for a time, but only love can change your heart. When you begin to see yourself through God's compassionate eyes, you no longer avoid sin merely because of its consequences. You turn away from it because you recognize that it wounds the relationship for which you were created. Sin is not terrible because God delights in punishment. It is terrible because it separates the soul from the joy of living in love. I was shown no suffering more painful than this. A soul made for communion with God has no deeper sorrow than to live apart from that communion.

Yet even here, the Lord teaches you how to see rightly. Hate the sin with all your heart, but never hate the soul—neither your own nor another's. God never confuses the two, and neither should you. He opposes everything that diminishes love, yet His love for the person never falters. If you condemn yourself without mercy, you are seeing yourself differently than God sees you. If you despise another because of their failures, you are forgetting how patiently Christ has loved you. The work of grace is not merely to make you righteous, but to teach you to love as God loves.

So let your heart become like His. Be honest about sin, but even more honest about mercy. Resist whatever draws you away from love, yet never lose sight of the dignity God has placed within every human soul. Learn to see yourself and your neighbor through the eyes of Christ, whose love is never broken by our failures. His promise still stands: **I keep you securely.** Rest in that promise. From that place of safety, let love do its quiet work within you until you, too, hate only what wounds love and cherish all whom Love has made.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Teach me to love.*

> **Exhale:** *As You love me.*
