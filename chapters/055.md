## Day 55 – Christ Is Your Way Home

> *The One who walks beside you is already carrying you into the joy that awaits you.*

Christ is not merely the one who shows you the way. He is your Way. He has taken your humanity into Himself, and He bears it even now into the presence of the Father. I was shown that all who shall be saved are gathered within Christ as members of one living body. He presents us to the Father with delight, not as strangers seeking admission, but as those already beloved. The Father receives us with joy and returns us to the Son in an endless exchange of love. This holy giving and receiving is the gladness of heaven itself, and our Lord desires that we should begin to rejoice in it even now.

Though your feet still walk upon the earth, do not believe that earth is your deepest dwelling. Your life is hidden in a place far more real than anything your eyes can see. I was shown that we are more truly in heaven than we are in this passing world. Much of your heart still feels the weight of sorrow, uncertainty, and change, yet your truest life is already held secure in God. Faith is the quiet confidence that what is most real is often hidden from your senses. The soul learns to live from this unseen reality, trusting that heaven is not only your future home but the deepest truth of your present life.

From the first moment your soul was joined to your body, mercy began its gentle work. Grace did not wait until you became wise enough, good enough, or faithful enough. God came to dwell within you from the beginning, making your soul His chosen city. He has never departed from that holy dwelling, nor will He ever abandon it. Even when you feel empty, He remains full. Even when you feel lost, He has not lost you. The place Christ has taken within your soul is not one He will someday leave. His faithfulness is far steadier than your awareness of it.

As you grow through the seasons of life, God patiently unfolds within you the gifts He has already planted there. Just as a child slowly grows into the fullness of maturity, so your soul is quietly being formed for eternal life. Grace does not rush this work. Mercy accompanies every stage of it. There are times when you see only your unfinishedness, yet God sees the whole beauty He is bringing forth. Nothing that He has planted in love is wasted. Every hidden work of the Spirit prepares you for the fullness of life that has always been your destiny.

I was also shown a beautiful mystery: your soul bears the imprint of the Holy Trinity. You were made for communion because you were fashioned by communion itself. Your deepest life reflects the love, wisdom, and goodness of the God who made you. Though sin has wounded your experience of this truth, it has not destroyed its foundation. Christ has taken your humanity into His own so that every part of you—even what has known suffering, weakness, and death—might be restored. He entered our mortality so that nothing truly human would remain beyond the reach of resurrection.

So when you grow weary on the journey, remember that you are not walking toward God alone. Christ Himself is your path, your companion, and your destination. He carries your life within His own, leading you gently toward the home where you have always belonged. Trust the quiet work of grace. Trust the faithful presence within you. And when you cannot yet see the fullness of what God is doing, rest in this: the One who began your journey is already preparing your joyful arrival.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Christ, You are my way.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me home.*
