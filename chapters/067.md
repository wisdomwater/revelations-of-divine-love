## Day 67 – The City Within

> *The deepest place within you has always been God's beloved dwelling.*

I was shown something so beautiful that it has remained a source of wonder ever since. The Lord opened the eyes of my spirit and invited me to look within my own soul. What I expected to be small and hidden appeared instead as a vast and glorious kingdom, spacious beyond measure, radiant with quiet majesty. At its very center sat Christ Himself, enthroned in perfect peace. He was not a visitor who had come for a little while, nor a guest waiting to be welcomed. He was at home. I understood then that the place Jesus has chosen within the soul He will never abandon. It is His dwelling forever. If you wish to know where God most delights to be, do not look first to the heights of heaven or the wonders of creation. Look to the sanctuary He has lovingly prepared within you.

This changed the way I understood both God and myself. Before ever you desired Him, He desired you. Before you learned to love Him, He had already made His home within the soul He created. The Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit delighted together in bringing you into being. Nothing about your existence was accidental or reluctant. You were imagined in love, fashioned in wisdom, and called into life with joy. If God could have made your soul more beautiful, more noble, or more capable of sharing His life, He would have done so. Yet I was shown that He is fully pleased with the work of His hands. Even now, beneath every wound you carry and every burden you bear, He beholds the beauty He placed within you from the beginning.

Because your soul is so great, nothing smaller than God can ever satisfy it. We often ask created things to give us what only the Creator can provide. We seek lasting peace in success, possessions, admiration, certainty, or comfort, and for a little while they may seem enough. Yet before long the old hunger returns. This is not because the world is without goodness. It is because your soul was made for something infinitely greater. Even turning inward is not enough if you stop with yourself. You are not called to admire your own soul as though it were the destination. The soul is a holy city because God dwells there. Its greatest light is not its own beauty but the glory of the One who fills it with His presence.

Whenever your heart becomes heavy with the troubles of this world, remember where your truest life is found. The noise around you may be loud, and the sorrow you carry may feel deep, but neither reaches the innermost sanctuary where Christ reigns in unshaken peace. There is a stillness beneath every storm, a place where fear cannot rule because Love already sits upon the throne. Return there often. Not by striving, but by gently remembering who lives within you. The kingdom of God is nearer than your own breath because its King has never departed.

Lift your heart, then, above every passing grief that would persuade you your life is small or forgotten. You are the beloved dwelling place of God. The Lord who sustains the stars has chosen to make His home in you, and He delights in His choice. Rest in that wonder. Let it quiet your anxious thoughts and strengthen your weary spirit. For the One who reigns within your soul is also the One who holds all things together, and His presence in you is not temporary but everlasting. There, in the deepest place of your being, He waits with unchanging peace, inviting you to share forever in the joy He has always found in you.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Christ dwells within me.*

> Exhale: *I rest in Your peace.*
