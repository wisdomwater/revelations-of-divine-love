## Day 36 – Turn Your Heart Toward Me

> *When your eyes return to God, hope returns with them.*

The Lord showed me that He is accomplishing a work that no human hands could ever complete. Left to myself, I am all too capable of wandering, failing, and falling into sin. Yet even my weakness cannot prevent the goodness of God from reaching its perfect end. His love does not depend upon my strength. His faithfulness is not undone by my failures. This is not permission to take sin lightly, but an invitation to take God's grace more seriously. The final victory belongs not to human effort, but to divine love working faithfully through every season of our lives.

There are works of God that you can already see, and there are greater works still unfolding beyond your sight. The Lord gives glimpses of what is to come, not to satisfy every question, but to awaken hope. He reveals enough for your heart to trust Him, while keeping enough hidden that you continue to walk by faith. Receive both His revelations and His silences with gratitude. What He shows you is given because it is good for you now. What He conceals is held safely until the day when joy will be able to receive it in full.

Whenever your thoughts become consumed with failure—your own or someone else's—the Lord gently redirects your gaze. I felt His tender invitation within my soul, and I believe He speaks the same words to you: *My beloved child, let these thoughts rest. Turn toward Me. I am enough for you. Find your joy not in your fears, but in your Savior and in the salvation I am working within you.* How often we fix our attention upon what is broken until we can no longer see the One who is healing it. Yet Christ never tires of turning our faces back toward Himself. There, hope begins again.

Do not lose heart when sorrow or hardship comes before joy. I was shown that this has often been God's way. Before new life appears, there is waiting. Before healing is seen, there is aching. Before miracles become visible, there is often weakness that teaches us how deeply we need Him. This is not because God delights in suffering, but because He gently leads us beyond trust in ourselves into trust in His power. Every storm prepares the heart to recognize His faithfulness when it appears. So remain steadfast through the winds that now surround you. The same Lord who has worked wonders throughout every age continues His work even now. He has not forgotten you. His goodness is already moving toward a joy greater than you can yet imagine, and in His perfect time you will see that His love has been carrying you all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *You are enough for me.*

> **Exhale:** *I turn my heart to You.*
