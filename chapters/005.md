## Day 5 – You Are Enough

> *Everything that exists is held together by love.*

Our Lord once placed before the eyes of my soul something so small it rested easily in the palm of my hand. It was no larger than a hazelnut, and yet I understood that it represented all creation. I wondered how something so little could continue to exist. It seemed so fragile that it might disappear in an instant. Then I was given a simple answer that has never left me: it endures because God loves it. Every created thing draws its very life from that love. Nothing remains by its own strength. Everything is held, sustained, and cherished by the One who brought it into being.

If this is true of the whole world, how much more is it true of you? There may be days when you feel small, unnoticed, or uncertain of your place. You may wonder whether your life matters amid the vastness of creation. Remember what I was shown. The God who fashioned the stars also delights to hold what seems small. Your life is not preserved because you have earned your place or proved your worth. You continue because you are loved. Before you accomplish anything, before anyone sees you, love has already called you into being and faithfully keeps you.

I also came to see that our hearts become restless whenever we ask created things to carry what only God can bear. We search for lasting peace in success, possessions, relationships, certainty, or comfort. These are good gifts, but they are too small to become our home. They were never meant to carry the weight of our deepest longing. Only the One who made all things can satisfy the soul that was made for Him. This is not because creation is lacking, but because every beautiful thing points beyond itself to the Love from which it came.

So let your prayer become wonderfully simple. You need not gather many words or search for perfect requests. Bring your empty hands and your honest heart. Dare to ask for nothing less than God Himself. Every lesser gift will leave you wanting something more, but the Giver contains every good thing. When your soul learns to rest there, you will discover a peace that does not depend upon changing circumstances. You will find that the deepest prayer has always been this: "Lord, give me Yourself, for in You I already have all."

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are enough.*
>
> Exhale: *In You I rest.*
