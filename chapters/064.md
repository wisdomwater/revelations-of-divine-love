## Day 64 – You Shall Come Up Above

> *Every sorrow has an ending, but the love that awaits you never does.*

There have been seasons when I longed to leave this world behind. I saw so much weariness, both within myself and around me, that my heart ached for the fullness of God's presence. There were days when my own weakness seemed too great, when the journey felt longer than I wished to walk, and when the absence of my Lord was itself a sorrow deeper than any outward suffering. If you have ever felt this longing—if you have ever grown tired of carrying burdens that seem too heavy—you need not be ashamed. Bring even this longing into the light of God's love, for He receives it with great tenderness.

In my longing, the Lord answered me with words that have remained a lasting comfort: **"You shall be taken from all your pain. You shall come up above. You shall have Me, and you shall be filled with love and joy."** These words were not spoken only for me, but for every soul that grows weary along the road. There is coming a day when every grief will end, every wound will be healed, and every restless desire will find its rest in God. Until then, you are invited to trust what you cannot yet see. Your present suffering is not the final chapter of your story. It is only a passing moment beside the endless joy that is being prepared for you.

I was shown a body lying upon the earth, heavy, broken, and without beauty. Then, in an instant, there sprang from it a radiant little child, pure, living, and dazzling with light, who rose swiftly into heaven. I understood that this frail body, with all its weakness and pain, cannot imprison the soul that belongs to God. Your deepest self is not defined by illness, disappointment, failure, or age. Beneath everything that weighs upon you lives a life that Christ is already preparing for glory. What now feels burdened will one day be light. What now seems hidden will shine with a beauty that has always been held safely within the love of God.

Because this promise is true, do not let today's troubles claim more of your heart than they deserve. I do not say this to make light of your suffering. God Himself looks upon your pain with profound compassion. He knows every tear before it falls. Yet He asks you to hold your sorrows in the larger light of His promises. When your heart can glimpse, even for a moment, the joy that awaits you, your present burdens begin to lose some of their power. They remain real, but they are no longer ultimate. The horizon of eternity changes the meaning of every road that leads toward it.

Whenever your spirit grows heavy again—as it surely will from time to time—return to this promise. Do not imagine that God has forgotten you simply because the darkness has returned. His love has not changed. His purpose has not faltered. Lift your thoughts, as often as grace allows, toward the joy that is coming. Let your hope become larger than your fear, and let His promises become weightier than your pain. For the day is surely coming when every sorrow will fall away forever, and you will discover that Christ Himself has been your reward all along. Then you shall understand with perfect clarity that every step of the journey has been leading you upward into the endless embrace of divine love.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are my hope.*

> Exhale: *You will bring me home.*
