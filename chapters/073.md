## Day 73 – Learning to Trust Love

> *The soul is healed not by becoming stronger than its weakness, but by trusting the love that is stronger still.*

All that I have been shown has come in many ways. Some things were placed before my eyes. Some were spoken quietly within my understanding. Others were given in a deeper seeing that words can scarcely contain. Yet through every vision and every word, one desire of God remained constant: that you should know His love and rest securely within it. Among all the ways sin troubles the soul, I was shown two hidden sicknesses that most often burden those who sincerely long to follow Christ. They do not belong chiefly to those who have turned away from God, but to those who earnestly seek Him and yet grow weary along the way. Because these sicknesses often disguise themselves as virtue, they can remain hidden unless we learn to recognize them.

The first is impatience. We become discouraged by the slow work of grace. We wish our struggles were over, our hearts were purer, and our love more constant than it is. We grow tired of carrying the same burdens and wonder why God has not finished His work in us already. Yet our Lord gently turned my eyes toward His own Passion. He bore suffering without resentment, trusting that love would accomplish what force never could. In the same way, He invites you to endure your own hardships with quiet patience. The slow work of love is never wasted. Every trial received in trust becomes another place where grace quietly takes root.

The second sickness lies even deeper. It is the fearful heart that cannot fully believe how good God truly is. Many believe without hesitation that God is all-powerful. Many gladly confess that He is perfectly wise. Yet when it comes to believing that He is unfailingly loving—that He truly wills your healing, your joy, and your salvation—many hearts begin to hesitate. This hidden mistrust becomes the source of countless sorrows. We look upon our failures, our repeated sins, and our broken promises until shame grows larger than mercy. We imagine this heaviness is humility, but I was shown that it is not. It is a subtle temptation to doubt the goodness of the One who has already forgiven us.

Do not hold against yourself what God has already released. When you have turned again toward Him, receive His forgiveness with the same sincerity with which He offers it. His desire is not only to forgive your sins but also to free you from the burden of carrying them after they have been forgiven. Love asks you to stop arguing with mercy. The enemy keeps your eyes fixed upon your weakness; Christ gently lifts your gaze toward His heart. There you will discover that His power is never harsh, His wisdom is never cold, and His love makes both power and wisdom wonderfully gentle.

Therefore, whenever you grow discouraged by your own frailty, remember what I have learned above all else: the greatest remedy is to know Love. Return there again and again. Let God's love become more convincing than your shame, more familiar than your fears, and more trustworthy than your own thoughts. The soul that rests in Love finds the courage to begin again each day. And as you learn to forgive yourself because God has forgiven you, you will discover that grace has been quietly healing you all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your love is greater.*

> Exhale: *I rest in Your mercy.*
