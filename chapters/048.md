## Day 48 – Mercy Keeps You, Grace Raises You

> *Mercy meets you where you fall; grace lifts you higher than you imagined possible.*

This life is full of change. There are days when your heart is steady and days when it feels divided. You long for peace, yet you find yourself pulled in many directions. Still, the Holy Spirit never ceases His quiet work within you. He dwells faithfully in your soul, gently calming what has become restless, softening what has grown hard, and drawing your heart back into harmony with God. This is the patient work of divine mercy. Even when you are scarcely aware of it, Love is already restoring what fear has unsettled.

I was shown that the anger we so often imagine in God does not belong to Him. What separates us from peace is not His wrath toward us, but our own resistance to His love. Whenever we cling to fear, pride, or despair, we experience the painful distance created by our own divided hearts. Yet God's gaze never turns away. Even in our wandering, His eyes remain fixed upon us with tenderness and compassion. He does not abandon us while we are weak. Mercy continues its work without growing weary, patiently turning us again toward the life for which we were created.

The Lord helped me see two beautiful movements within His one great love: mercy and grace. Mercy is like the embrace of a loving mother. It keeps you, bears with you, heals you, and gently restores what has been wounded. Grace is like the generous gift of a sovereign king. It does more than repair what was broken. It lifts you beyond what you ever thought possible. Mercy binds your wounds; grace crowns your healing. Mercy steadies your trembling feet; grace teaches you to walk with joy. Together they reveal a love that never stops at forgiveness but always leads toward fullness of life.

So do not measure your life only by the pain you now endure. There are sorrows whose purpose you cannot yet understand and struggles that seem only to diminish you. Yet grace is quietly at work within them, preparing a joy that will one day surpass every grief. You will not forever remember your wounds as wounds. In the light of God's presence, you will see how mercy faithfully carried you and how grace transformed every loss into deeper life. Then your heart will overflow with thanksgiving—not because suffering was good in itself, but because Love refused to waste a single tear. Until that day, entrust yourself to the One whose mercy never ceases and whose grace is always leading you beyond your present weakness into everlasting joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Your mercy keeps me.*

> **Exhale:** *Your grace raises me.*
