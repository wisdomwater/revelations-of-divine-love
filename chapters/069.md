## Day 69 – Holding Fast to Love

> *The deepest victory is not found in never being tested, but in refusing to let go of love.*

There are times when darkness seems to gather around the soul. Fear becomes louder than peace. Doubts crowd the mind. Prayer feels distracted, and every good thing appears distant. In such moments, it is easy to imagine that God has withdrawn or that you have somehow fallen beyond His reach. I, too, passed through such a night. I was troubled by thoughts that sought to draw me toward despair, and all around me seemed filled with confusion and unrest. Yet I learned that the greatest battle is not against darkness itself, but against the temptation to believe that darkness speaks the truth.

When these fears surrounded me, I did not overcome them by my own strength. Instead, I turned again toward the One who had already revealed His faithfulness. I fixed my eyes upon the cross, remembered the suffering of Christ, confessed the faith that had been entrusted to me, and fastened my heart to God with all the trust I could gather. I even spoke words of comfort to my own soul, as I would have spoken to a dear friend in distress. Sometimes this is what faith looks like—not the absence of fear, but the quiet decision to keep returning your attention to divine love whenever fear demands your gaze.

As I endured that long night, another understanding was given to me. I realized that if I devoted as much care to remaining rooted in love as I often gave to my fears, my soul would know a far greater freedom. We spend so much of our strength guarding ourselves against imagined dangers, yet the truest safety is found in remaining close to Christ. A heart that continually turns toward love becomes less vulnerable to the voices that would lead it away from hope. This is not because temptation disappears, but because love becomes stronger than the temptation to despair.

By morning the darkness had passed. It left behind only the fading memory of its presence. I saw then how powerless it truly was. Evil can trouble the mind, but it cannot possess the soul that rests in Christ. It may make much noise, but it cannot silence the voice of divine love. It may linger for a season, but it cannot endure forever. The victory had never belonged to the darkness. It belonged to Christ long before the struggle began. His Passion had already broken the power of everything that sought to separate me from God.

Remember this whenever your own nights feel long. Do not measure the strength of God's love by the intensity of your struggle. Lift your eyes again to Christ. Speak His promises to your heart. Hold fast, even if your faith feels no larger than a whisper. The One who conquered sin and death is already at work within you. His victory is not merely a story from the past; it is a living strength that carries you through every trial. And when the darkness finally passes—as it surely will—you will discover that it was never your fear that defined you, but the faithful love that held you through it all.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Christ, hold my heart.*

> Exhale: *Your love is my victory.*
