## Day 27 – All Shall Be Well

> *Where you cannot yet understand, you may still trust Love.*

There was a question that lived quietly within me for many years. If God is all-wise and all-loving, why was sin ever allowed to enter the world? Why was the first wound not prevented before it could spread through every human life? It seemed to me that if sin had never been, all would have remained beautiful and whole. I carried this question with sorrow, believing there must be an answer I had not yet found. Then the Lord answered me—not with an explanation, but with a promise: **"It behoved that there should be sin; but all shall be well, and all shall be well, and all manner of thing shall be well."**

These words did not make light of suffering. I was shown the grief that sin has brought into the world—the wounds we inflict upon one another, the burdens we carry within ourselves, the pain that reaches through every generation. None of this was hidden from me. Yet neither was I left there. The vision passed swiftly into comfort, for the Lord would not have the soul dwell in terror. He wanted me to see that pain is real, but it is not ultimate. The deepest truth is not the brokenness of creation but the faithfulness of the One who will heal it.

I also understood something that changed the way I looked at sin itself. I did not see it as a thing with lasting substance. I saw only the suffering it causes. Sin is like a shadow that has no life of its own. It wounds, it distorts, it deceives, but it cannot endure forever because it has no foundation in the being of God. Love is real. Mercy is real. Goodness is real. Sin is known only by the pain it leaves behind, and even that pain, held within the hands of Christ, becomes a place where we learn humility, discover our need for mercy, and are gently drawn back toward Love.

So when you are troubled by the evil you see in the world—or by the failures you see within yourself—do not imagine that God stands over you in accusation. I was shown no blame in His face, only immeasurable tenderness. He knows your weakness more fully than you do, yet His purpose has never changed. There remains a mystery we cannot yet see, a wisdom too vast for this life alone. One day it will be unveiled, and what now seems impossible to reconcile will become a cause for endless praise. Until that day, hold fast to the words that have carried my own soul through every unanswered question: **All shall be well. All shall be well. All manner of thing shall be well.**

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *All shall be well.*

> Exhale: *I trust Your love.*
