## Day 63 – Becoming What You Were Made to Be

> *Grace does not erase your true nature; it lovingly restores it.*

I have been shown that everything God has made is beautiful in its deepest nature. Sin does not belong to who you truly are. It is not the foundation of your being, nor the truest story of your life. It is a wound, a distortion, something foreign that has settled where it was never meant to remain. This is why your heart feels restless whenever it clings to what is false. You were created for love, truth, and communion with God. Whenever you turn away from these, something within you knows that you have wandered from home. Do not mistake this sorrow for rejection. It is the quiet witness of the life God planted within you, calling you back to what is most truly yourself.

Nature and grace are never at war. The God who created you is the same God who restores you. Grace does not come to replace your humanity but to heal it. Just as a skilled physician patiently tends a broken bone until it becomes strong again, so Christ lovingly tends every place where sin has wounded the soul. He works without haste, yet never without purpose. Day by day He softens what has grown hard, cleanses what has become stained, and awakens what has fallen asleep. His work is often quieter than you expect, but it is no less certain. Love never abandons what love has made.

So do not be afraid when you become aware of your faults. Let that awareness lead you toward God rather than away from Him. Bring your sorrow to Christ with the trust of a child who runs into the arms of a loving mother after falling. There is no wound He cannot touch with mercy. There is no stain His love cannot wash away. His healing is sometimes slow because He desires not merely to remove the pain, but to restore the whole person with wisdom and tenderness. Be patient with the pace of grace. The One who began this work will not rest until every part of you reflects the beauty He has always seen.

I was shown that the truest posture of the soul is not mastery, but childlikeness. A child does not imagine that life depends upon its own strength. A child trusts, receives, and rests in the love of another. Such humility is not weakness but wisdom. The child does not despair of the mother's love, nor boast in its own ability. It delights in being cared for and learns to love its brothers and sisters as members of one family. These are the virtues that gladden the heart of God: trust instead of fear, humility instead of pride, and love instead of rivalry. As long as you remain in this life, do not be ashamed to be a little child before your heavenly Mother.

One day the work of grace will be complete. What now seems hidden will become wonderfully clear. Then you will understand why I was given those unforgettable words: **All shall be well.** You will see for yourself that God's love has never ceased guiding creation toward its true fulfillment. Every wound will be healed. Every distortion will be made beautiful. Every child born of divine love will be gathered home by divine grace. And that homecoming will not be an ending, but the beginning of a joy that is forever new, forever deepening, forever unfolding within the endless love of God.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Heal what is wounded.*

> **Exhale:** *Restore me in love.*
