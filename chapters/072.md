## Day 72 – The Longing That Leads You Home

> *The deepest longing of your soul is the quiet invitation of God drawing you to Himself.*

There is a longing within you that nothing in this world can finally satisfy. You may try to quiet it with success, comfort, beauty, friendship, or even the holiest of earthly gifts, yet it continues to whisper beneath them all. Do not mistake this longing for emptiness alone. It is the echo of the home for which you were created. Your soul was made to behold the radiant face of God, and until that day comes in its fullness, every joy you experience here remains a promise of something greater still. The hunger itself is holy because it is drawing you toward the One who alone can fulfill it.

I was shown that nothing is more opposed to this blessed vision than sin. Not because God withdraws His love from you, but because sin clouds your sight. It is like a thick mist settling over a beautiful landscape. The sun has not ceased to shine, yet you cannot see it clearly. So it is with the soul. Whenever we cling to what is false, selfish, or unloving, our vision grows dim. We feel separated from the joy we were made for. Yet even then, God has not departed. His love has not diminished. He remains nearer to you than your own breath, faithfully holding you until the mist begins to lift. Though you may feel far from Him, you are never beyond His keeping.

This is why your heart continues to ache for Him. Even now Christ surrounds you completely. He dwells within you, embraces you with immeasurable tenderness, and refuses to let you go. Still, there remains within the soul a holy yearning that will not rest until it sees Him face to face. Do not fear this yearning or try to silence it. It is one of grace's greatest gifts. It reminds you that this present life, beautiful as it can be, is not your final home. Every tear shed for God, every quiet ache for deeper communion, every prayer that rises from love rather than mere duty is already part of your journey into His everlasting joy.

There are days when this longing feels like grief. Your prayers seem dry. Your faith feels clouded. You wonder why God appears hidden. Yet I have learned that this hiddenness often awakens a deeper desire than constant consolation ever could. If every earthly comfort were placed into your hands, your heart would still quietly search for the face of the One who gave them. And if, in the midst of great suffering, you were granted even a glimpse of His radiant countenance, you would discover a joy that reaches deeper than pain itself. Such is the beauty of Christ. His presence does not merely lessen sorrow—it transforms it.

All that I have been shown has gently led me to three simple truths. First, know your Lord, whose love is deeper than you have yet imagined. Second, know yourself as one created in His image, cherished by both nature and grace. Third, know your weakness with humility, not so that you may despair, but so that you may rest more completely in His mercy. Hold these three together, and they will guide your steps. For the more clearly you know God, the more truthfully you will know yourself, and the more fully you know yourself, the more deeply you will trust the love that has been seeking you from the very beginning. In that love, every longing finds its fulfillment, and every tear becomes joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are my deepest desire.*

> Exhale: *Lead me into Your joy.*
