## Day 79 – Turn Quickly Toward Love

> *Do not linger in your failure; turn quickly toward the One who has never turned away from you.*

I was first shown my own weakness before I understood that it belonged not only to me, but to all humanity. In time I came to see that I stand within the great family of souls who are learning, falling, rising, and being healed by the same mercy. This changed the way I looked upon others. I was no longer called to occupy myself with their failures, but with my own need for grace. If I ever see another's weakness, let it be only that I may pray more tenderly, love more deeply, and encourage more gently. Love has never asked you to become the judge of another soul. It asks you to become its companion.

This understanding also taught me a holy humility. I do not know where I might stumble if left to myself. I cannot measure my own strength, nor should I trust in it. Such knowledge is not meant to fill the heart with anxiety but with dependence. When you know your need for God, you become less likely to presume upon yourself and more ready to lean upon His faithfulness. Humility is not thinking that you are beyond hope. It is knowing that your hope has never rested in yourself to begin with.

Yet alongside this humility, I was shown something even more wonderful: the love of God for your soul will never be divided from you. His love is steadfast, unchanging, and stronger than every season of your life. Hold these two truths together. Let the awareness of your weakness protect you from pride, and let the certainty of His love protect you from despair. If either is separated from the other, your heart will lose its balance. Humility without love becomes discouragement. Love without humility becomes carelessness. But together they lead you into the freedom of trust.

Be careful, then, not to misunderstand the greatness of God's mercy. If the knowledge of His love ever tempts you to become careless with your life or indifferent toward sin, you have wandered from the truth. Love never makes us careless. It awakens gratitude. It softens the heart. It teaches us to treasure the One who treasures us. Whenever you fall through weakness or forgetfulness, do not remain there accusing yourself endlessly. The Lord will gently show you where you have wandered, but He has no desire for you to build your home in regret. He reveals your need only so that you may return.

Therefore, when you become aware of your sin, turn without delay toward Christ. Do not spend your strength rehearsing your failures. Do not believe that endless self-accusation pleases God. He waits for you with a longing greater than your own. He stands ready, not in anger, but in patient love, eager to gather you again into His embrace. You are His joy and His delight, just as He is your healing and your life. Every moment you turn back to Him is already the beginning of restoration. His heart has been facing you all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I turn to You.*

> Exhale: *You are my life.*
