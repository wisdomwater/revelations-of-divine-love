## Day 53 – The Will That Still Belongs to God

> *Beneath every wound, there remains a place in you that has never ceased to belong to God.*

Do not think that God receives your failures with harsher judgment than He received the fall of Adam. The One who loved humanity from the beginning has never ceased to love what He has made. His love is not surprised by your weakness, nor diminished by your wandering. I was shown that God is so gentle and so full of courtesy that He does not delight in assigning blame to those whom He is preparing for everlasting joy. He sees farther than you can see. Where you often behold only your falling, He already beholds your restoration.

I was also given a great comfort that quieted many of my questions. I saw that within every soul that shall be saved there is a godly will that has never consented to sin, nor ever shall. Beneath all your confusion, beneath every fear, beneath every false desire that has troubled your mind, there remains a deeper place where your true life rests in God. This deepest willing was never created for evil. It was formed only for love, and even when you lose sight of it, God never does. What often feels hidden from you remains perfectly known to Him.

Perhaps there are days when you scarcely recognize yourself. Your thoughts accuse you. Your habits disappoint you. Your heart seems divided against itself. Yet do not mistake these passing storms for the truest thing about you. They are real, and they must be faced honestly, but they do not reach the deepest foundation of your soul. There is a place within you where grace has quietly kept watch from the beginning, a place where your life remains hidden with Christ. The more you learn to dwell from this deeper center, the more gently you will hold your own struggles, and the more patiently you will trust the slow work of God within you.

Know this also: God did not begin loving you on the day you were born, nor when you first believed, nor after you learned to love Him in return. Before He made you, He loved you. Before your first breath, before your first joy, before your first sorrow, you were already known. You existed first in the loving intention of God. Your life is not an afterthought or an accident. You have always been held within the eternal delight of the One who imagined you before time itself. And when you came into being, your soul was fashioned for communion with the God who had already loved it without beginning.

Your soul was made from nothing that was created. God gathered the earth to form your body, but your soul came directly from His creative love. Because of this, there is nothing that truly belongs between your soul and its Maker. Many things may seem to separate you—fear, shame, distraction, grief—but these are clouds passing before the sun. They are not the bond itself. The deepest truth is that your life is held within the endless love from which it came and toward which it is always being drawn.

Carry this remembrance with you today. When you are tempted to define yourself by your failures, look deeper. When shame tells you that you have become something less than beloved, listen instead for the quiet voice beneath every accusation. There you will find the will that still longs for God because it has never ceased to belong to Him. Trust that hidden work. Nurture it with prayer, with gratitude, and with hope. The One who loved you before you existed is even now bringing you toward the joy for which you were created, and His love will not fail before its work is complete.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You have always loved me.*

> Exhale: *My soul rests in You.*
