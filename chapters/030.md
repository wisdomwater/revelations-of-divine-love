## Day 30 – Rest in What Has Been Revealed

> *Peace grows where trust no longer demands every answer.*

There are many things you long to understand. You wonder why certain prayers seem unanswered, why suffering follows one path instead of another, why the story of the world unfolds as it does. I have asked such questions myself. Yet the Lord showed me that there are two kinds of knowing. One has been opened to us with great generosity. The other remains hidden within the wisdom of God. The first is given so that we may live. The second is withheld so that we may learn to trust.

What has been opened is more than enough for the soul. Christ has shown you the heart of God. He has revealed that divine love seeks your salvation, your healing, and your everlasting joy. He has shown you mercy stronger than sin, compassion deeper than sorrow, and a faithfulness that never abandons what it has made. Here the light is clear. Here there is no need to search through shadows. The Lord invites you to dwell in this truth, to return to it again and again, allowing it to become the steady ground beneath your feet.

There are, however, mysteries that belong to God alone. Why one life unfolds differently from another, why certain sorrows are permitted, how every thread of history will finally be gathered into perfect goodness—these things remain within His loving wisdom. I was shown that much of our weariness comes from trying to force open doors that God has not yet chosen to unlock. We spend our strength chasing hidden answers while neglecting the beautiful truth already placed into our hands. Yet love loses nothing by waiting. The mysteries of God are not hidden because He delights in secrecy, but because He knows the soul must first be rooted in trust before it can bear greater light.

So let your heart rest where Christ has invited it to rest. Rejoice in what has already been revealed instead of being consumed by what remains concealed. Whenever unanswered questions begin to trouble your spirit, turn again to Jesus. He is not merely one answer among many. He is the place where every faithful question may safely come to rest. In time, all that needs to be known will be made known. Until then, receive with gratitude what Love has already shown you, and entrust the hidden things to the gentle wisdom of the One who has never ceased to lead you toward everlasting joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I trust Your wisdom.*

> Exhale: *I rest in Your love.*
