## Day 60 – The Kind, Loving Mother

> *The love that gave you birth never ceases to nourish you.*

I was shown that the love which created you is the very same love that restores you. God's motherly care does not begin only after you have wandered or fallen. It has always surrounded you. The love that first brought you into being remains faithful through every season of your life, gently leading you back to the place for which you were made. Divine love never abandons its own work. What begins in love continues in love until it reaches its joyful fulfillment.

Our gracious Mother desired to come as near to us as love itself could come. Therefore Christ clothed Himself in our humanity, entering the lowliness of Mary's womb with perfect humility. The One through whom all things were made chose the smallest beginning, not because His greatness was diminished, but because love delights to draw near. In taking our flesh, He embraced the whole work of motherhood. He did not stand above our weakness. He entered it. He did not save us from a distance. He carried us within Himself.

Every loving mother bears her child through pain into life. Yet even this earthly mystery is only a faint reflection of Christ's greater love. Our true Mother bore us through the anguish of the cross into everlasting life. He carried us through suffering so that we might be born into joy. And I was shown that even when His Passion had accomplished all that love required, His desire to love us had not come to an end. If there had been more that love could have given, love would have given more. Such is the measureless generosity of the heart of Christ.

Because His love does not cease, neither does His care. A mother does not give birth and then forget her child. She feeds, comforts, strengthens, and remains near. So our Lord continues to nourish us with Himself. He feeds us with His life, His presence, His Word, and every grace that awakens the soul. Whenever you are strengthened by truth, comforted in sorrow, forgiven in weakness, or quietly drawn toward God, Christ is feeding you. Every true gift that nourishes your spirit flows from His endless tenderness.

As a child grows, a wise mother changes her care without changing her love. There are times for gentle holding and times for patient correction. There are seasons of consolation and seasons of discipline. Not because love has diminished, but because love seeks maturity. So it is with our Lord. There are hardships He permits, not to wound us, but to free us from all that keeps us from life. Even when the path feels difficult, His heart remains full of compassion. The hand that allows the pruning is the same hand that never lets us go.

At the end of all my seeing, I understood something wonderfully simple. Every love you have ever known that has been pure, patient, sacrificial, and life-giving has always been pointing toward Him. Every faithful father, every tender mother, every kind friend, every gentle guide reflects, however imperfectly, the inexhaustible love of God. And when your heart reaches out in love, it is Christ already at work within you. So hear His quiet words today as though spoken only to you: **It is I whom you love.** Every true longing, every sincere affection, every holy desire finds its home in Him. Rest there, and let your whole heart be gathered into the love that has carried you from the beginning and will carry you forever.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Loving Mother, nourish me.*

> Exhale: *I rest in Your care.*
