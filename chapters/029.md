## Day 29 – Greater Than the Wound

> *No wound is greater than the love that longs to heal it.*

Even after I heard the Lord promise that all would be well, my heart still carried its questions. I looked upon the sorrow of the world—the brokenness of human lives, the wounds left by sin, the grief that stretches across every generation—and I quietly asked, "How can this truly be? How can all be well when so much has been lost?" I did not ask from disbelief, but from longing. I wanted to see how such a promise could possibly embrace so much pain.

The Lord did not rebuke my question. He received it with gentleness. Love is never threatened by an honest heart. He reminded me that the deepest wound ever inflicted upon creation had already been answered. Humanity's first great turning away from God was no surprise to Him, nor was it beyond His power to heal. Then He invited me to stop looking only at the wound and to behold something greater still: the restoration He had accomplished through Christ. The remedy, He showed me, is more glorious than the injury. Grace is not merely equal to our brokenness—it surpasses it.

Then I understood the wisdom hidden within His promise. If divine love has already embraced the greatest harm the world has known, why should I doubt its power to heal every lesser wound? If Christ has entered the deepest darkness and filled it with His light, then no corner of my own life lies beyond His reach. The heart naturally fixes its gaze upon what is broken. Love gently lifts our eyes toward what is being restored. The final story is not written by sin but by mercy.

So when the world seems too fractured to mend, or your own life feels marked by losses that cannot be undone, remember what I was shown. Do not measure God's faithfulness by the size of the wound. Measure it by the greatness of His love. The same Lord who has already overcome the deepest rupture in creation is patiently making all things new. You may not yet see how every thread will be woven together, and neither did I. Yet His promise remains stronger than our understanding: if He has made well the greatest harm, He will surely make well all that is less. Rest your heart there, and let hope become stronger than fear.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your grace is greater.*

> Exhale: *I trust Your healing.*
