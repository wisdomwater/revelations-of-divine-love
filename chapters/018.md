## Day 18 – Love Suffers Together

> *Where love is deepest, no sorrow is ever borne alone.*

I was shown that the suffering of Christ was never His alone. Those who loved Him most were drawn into His sorrow, not because they could lessen His burden, but because love cannot remain untouched by the pain of the beloved. I saw this most clearly in His blessed Mother. Her heart was so united with His that every wound He bore echoed within her. The greater her love, the greater her grief. This is not the cruelty of love, but its nature. Love joins one life to another until joy is shared and sorrow is shared as well.

As I beheld this, I understood that all who truly loved Christ suffered with Him. Their deepest pain was not for themselves but for the One who had become their life and their joy. I knew this not merely as a thought but as something I felt within my own soul. To love Christ is to discover that your heart no longer beats only for your own concerns. It begins to rejoice with His joy and to grieve where love is wounded. Such sorrow is not a sign that love has failed. It is the evidence that love has taken root.

Then I was shown something even more wondrous. There is a hidden union between Christ and all creation. When He suffered, the whole world trembled with Him. The earth itself seemed to mourn. The heavens grew dim. Even those who did not know His name sensed that something beyond words had happened. Creation recognizes its Maker more deeply than we often realize. Everything that has received life from God is, in ways both seen and unseen, bound together in the mystery of divine love.

So do not be surprised when another's suffering moves you, or when the grief of the world seems to find its way into your own heart. You were not created to stand apart from one another. The love of Christ is always drawing lives together, teaching us to carry one another with tenderness instead of indifference. Yet remember this also: if sorrow can be shared, then so can hope. The same union that allows us to suffer together also allows us to rejoice together. The Love that held Christ through the cross now holds every wounded heart, weaving each tear into a greater communion that will one day be made complete in everlasting joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *We are one in love.*

> Exhale: *Carry us together.*
