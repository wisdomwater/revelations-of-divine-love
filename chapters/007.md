## Day 7 – The Courtesy of God

> *The highest love does not remain distant; it draws wonderfully near.*

As I beheld the suffering of Christ, my heart was filled not only with sorrow but with wonder. His wounds were real. His love was real. Yet what astonished me most was not the depth of His suffering, but the gentleness with which He came to me. I expected greatness to appear with overwhelming majesty. Instead, I found holiness clothed in kindness. The One who fashioned the stars did not stand far away, demanding that I climb toward Him. He came close with the warmth of a dear friend, welcoming me without hesitation. In that nearness, my soul found rest.

I have often thought of Mary, whose greatness was born from seeing two truths at once. She saw the immeasurable glory of God, and she saw her own smallness. Yet these did not lead her into fear or despair. They blossomed into humility, and humility became the doorway through which grace entered so abundantly. You need not make yourself greater in order to draw near to God. It is enough to stand honestly before Him, knowing both who He is and who you are. His love is never diminished by your littleness. Indeed, it delights in meeting you there.

What brings the deepest joy to the heart is not merely that God is powerful, but that God is wonderfully approachable. Imagine a great ruler who lays aside every barrier simply to sit beside one who has nothing to offer but an open heart. Such kindness would be treasured more than riches, because it gives not a gift but a relationship. This is the mystery I was shown. Christ does not simply bestow mercy from a distance. He shares His very presence. The highest has become the nearest. The Lord of all has become our brother, welcoming us into a friendship beyond anything we could have imagined.

There will be times when this nearness feels unmistakable, and there will be times when it seems hidden from your sight. Do not think that love has departed simply because the feeling has faded. Visions pass, emotions rise and fall, but faith quietly carries what the heart cannot always perceive. Trust is the gentle thread that joins every season together. Hold fast to it with hope and love, and you will discover that the Christ who once seemed hidden has been walking beside you all along, with the same unfailing courtesy that has always been His delight.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You draw near.*
>
> Exhale: *I welcome Your presence.*
