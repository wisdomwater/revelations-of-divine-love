## Day 56 – Finding Your Soul in God

> *The deepest journey into yourself is always a journey into God.*

There is a longing within you to know God, and this longing is good. Yet I was shown something that filled me with wonder: it is often easier to begin knowing God than to know your own soul. Your soul is hidden so deeply within God that it cannot be fully understood apart from Him. If you seek only yourself, you will soon become lost among your own thoughts and feelings. But if you seek God with a sincere heart, you will slowly discover the truth of who you are, because your life has always been enclosed within His.

So do not think of knowing yourself as turning endlessly inward. Rather, turn toward the One in whom your soul lives. Your truest self is not found by endless self-examination but by resting in the love that first called you into being. As your vision of God becomes clearer, your vision of yourself becomes gentler and more truthful. You begin to see yourself not through fear or shame, nor through pride or ambition, but through the quiet gaze of divine love. In that gaze, nothing essential is hidden, and nothing beloved is rejected.

I was also shown a truth that has never ceased to comfort me: God is nearer to you than your own soul. Before you become aware of Him, He is already present. Before you pray, He is already listening. Before you seek Him, He is already sustaining your very life. He is the ground upon which your soul rests, the strength by which it stands, and the endless love in which it has taken root. There is no place within you where God is absent. Even the places that feel empty to you are held within His faithful presence.

Still, this knowledge unfolds slowly. We remain pilgrims while we walk this earthly road, growing through longing, repentance, joy, and sorrow. There are parts of ourselves that are not yet fully awake. There are depths that only grace can uncover. Do not be discouraged by this unfinishedness. The Lord Himself is leading you into these hidden places with the same love that created you and the same mercy that redeemed you. He is never impatient with your growth. Every season, even the painful ones, becomes part of His gentle work of bringing your whole soul into the light.

I came to understand that all God's gifts flow together like streams from one spring. Your very existence is His gift. Mercy meets you in your weakness. Grace carries you beyond what you could ever accomplish alone. Reason helps you discern, love teaches you to see, and the quiet work of the Spirit gathers every part of your life into wholeness. None of these stand apart from the others. They are different expressions of the one goodness of God, continually drawing you toward the joy for which you were made.

So let your desire to know God and your desire to know yourself become one desire. You do not need to choose between them, for they lead to the same holy meeting place. Return often to the stillness where God quietly dwells within you. There, beneath every anxious thought and every restless striving, your soul already rests in Him. And as you learn to remain there, even for a little while, heaven begins to unfold within you long before you arrive there in fullness.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are nearer.*

> Exhale: *I rest in You.*
