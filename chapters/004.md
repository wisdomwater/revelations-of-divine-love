## Day 4 – The Nearness of Holy Love

> *The God who is beyond all things delights to draw near.*

As I gazed upon Christ, I saw His blood flowing fresh from beneath the crown of thorns, as though His Passion were unfolding before me in that very moment. It was not a distant memory, nor a story confined to another age. Love was present. Love was giving itself still. I came to understand that the suffering of Christ is not meant to awaken fear but gratitude, for every wound speaks the same quiet truth: there is no place your pain can carry you where divine love has not already gone before you.

Yet in the very same moment that I beheld His suffering, my heart was filled with indescribable joy. This surprised me, for sorrow and joy seemed to meet without diminishing one another. I understood then that wherever Christ is truly known, the fullness of God is present. The love that creates, sustains, and redeems the world is one love, shared perfectly in the mystery of the Trinity. You need not untangle that mystery before you can live within it. It is enough to know that the God who made you also keeps you, surrounds you, and invites you into everlasting joy.

What filled me with even greater wonder was not only God's greatness, but God's closeness. The One whom heaven cannot contain came near without hesitation to one as small and frail as I was. I could scarcely comprehend such humility. Even now, I hope you will never grow accustomed to this miracle. The Holy One does not wait for you to become worthy before drawing near. God delights to dwell with those who know their need. The distance we often imagine between ourselves and God exists far more in our fears than in God's heart.

Then my thoughts turned toward Mary, whose greatness was born not from power but from humility. She knew both the majesty of God and the smallness of her own place within creation, yet neither truth filled her with despair. Instead, her humility became spacious enough to receive the impossible. So it is with every willing heart. You do not need to become extraordinary before God can work through you. Simply remain open, reverent, and willing to say yes to love as it comes. The God who chose to dwell within Mary's humble life still delights to make a home within every heart that welcomes Him.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You draw near.*
>
> Exhale: *I welcome Your love.*
