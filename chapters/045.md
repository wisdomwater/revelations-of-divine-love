## Day 45 – See Yourself Through God's Eyes

> *God's deepest judgment is not condemnation, but the unchanging love with which He knows you.*

There are times when you look at yourself and see only your failures. You measure your life by your weaknesses, your regrets, and the ways you have not yet become the person you long to be. This is how the changing part of your life often appears. Yet the Lord showed me that He sees you more deeply than you see yourself. He beholds the truest substance of your soul—the life He created, holds, and keeps within His own love. That deepest self has never been separated from Him. It remains safe in His care, even while the restless surface of your life is still being healed.

This does not mean that your failings are ignored. God sees them with perfect clarity. Yet He sees them as a loving physician sees a wound—not as a reason to reject the one who suffers, but as something to be restored. Mercy is His way of bringing all that is broken into harmony with the goodness for which you were created. Christ does not stand over you to prove your unworthiness. He comes beside you to make you whole. Every act of grace is another step toward restoring your outward life to the truth that has always been held within God.

There are mysteries that your mind may not yet be able to reconcile. You know both the seriousness of sin and the boundless mercy of God, yet you wonder how these truths belong together. I carried this same longing within my own heart. I desired to understand how God's perfect justice and His perfect love could speak with one voice. The Lord did not answer every question at once. Instead, He invited me to remain within the mystery, trusting that what now appears divided will one day be seen as one. Some truths are learned not through explanation but through faithful waiting.

So be patient with yourself when your understanding feels incomplete. Continue to let God's gaze become your own. See your failures honestly, but never more clearly than you see His love. The more you recognize where you still need healing, the more deeply your heart will long for the fullness of life that awaits you. That longing is not a sign that you are far from God. It is evidence that His grace is already drawing you home. Your truest life is already hidden with Him, and one day every divided part of you will be gathered into perfect wholeness. Then you will know yourself as He has always known you: held, beloved, and forever at rest in His endless joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You know my true self.*

> Exhale: *Make me whole in love.*
