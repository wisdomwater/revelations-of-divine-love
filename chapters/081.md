## Day 81 – The Longing That Is Already Prayer

> *Your deepest longing for God is itself a quiet work of His love within you.*

I was shown that our Lord reveals Himself in many beautiful ways. He came among us in the humility of His birth. He made His love known through His suffering upon the cross. He fills all creation with His presence, so that even the smallest thing bears witness to Him. Yet of all the places where I saw Him dwell, none was dearer than the human soul. There He has chosen His home. There He has built His holy city. He has not entered your soul as a passing visitor but as its everlasting King. Once He has made His dwelling there, He will never abandon it. His delight is to remain with you forever.

Because this is true, I would have you answer His gentle movements within your heart with joy more than with sorrow. You know well your failures, and it is right to turn from them. But do not spend more time grieving over your weakness than rejoicing in His love. Our Lord showed me that nothing honors Him more than a soul that learns to live with quiet gladness because it trusts His goodness. This gladness is not careless laughter that ignores suffering. It is the deep joy of one who knows that love has already claimed the final victory. Even while your journey remains difficult, you may walk it with hope.

I came to understand something that surprised me greatly. God sees this whole earthly life as a kind of holy longing. The ache you carry for Him, the incompleteness you sometimes feel, the quiet yearning that no earthly happiness can entirely satisfy—He receives all of this as a precious offering. He knows that your heart was made for more than this passing world can give. Your longing is not a sign that something has gone wrong. It is the mark of a soul that remembers, however faintly, the home for which it was created. Even more wonderful, I was shown that God Himself longs for you. His love patiently waits until every one of His children is gathered into the fullness of His joy.

For now, wisdom and love have joined together in allowing this season of waiting. There are reasons hidden within God's goodness that you cannot yet fully see. Yet He does not leave you to bear the waiting alone. The very longing you experience is sustained by His presence. He carries it with you. He strengthens you beneath it. Every sigh for deeper communion, every prayer that rises from an unsatisfied heart, every quiet desire for holiness is already being held within His own desire for you.

Therefore, whenever your heart grows weary from the incompleteness of this life, gently lift your gaze beyond what you presently feel. Do not deny the pain, but do not let it become the horizon of your soul. Set your heart upon the joy that has been promised. Let hope become stronger than weariness. Let trust become deeper than disappointment. For the longing you carry will not last forever. The day is surely coming when desire will become fulfillment, faith will become sight, and the God who has made His home within you will welcome you into the home He has prepared for you. Then every longing will give way to everlasting joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Draw me to Yourself.*

> **Exhale:** *My hope is in You.*
