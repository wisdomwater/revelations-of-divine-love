## Day 77 – Rise Again with Joy

> *Your falling does not delight God, but your rising always does.*

I was shown that everything which leads you away from love and peace does not come from the heart of God. The voice that condemns without mercy, that whispers you are beyond hope, that urges you to give up—this is not the voice of your Lord. You will stumble because you are still growing. You will grow weary because you are still walking. Such weakness belongs to this earthly life. Yet every time you turn again toward God, His grace accomplishes something beautiful. What the enemy hoped would become your defeat becomes, by mercy, another step toward humility, compassion, and joy. Your rising brings greater glory to God than your falling ever brought sorrow.

Do not be surprised, then, if the struggle continues. The enemy would gladly have you believe that each failure proves you have made no progress at all. But I was shown the opposite. Whenever you rise again through love and humility, all that was meant for your harm is transformed into your healing. Even the darkness is made to serve the purposes of grace. The one who delights in accusation cannot bear to see a soul return to Christ with renewed trust. Every act of repentance weakens despair and strengthens love. Every return becomes another witness that mercy is greater than failure.

When you become aware of your weakness, do not accuse yourself beyond what truth requires. There is a sorrow that leads the heart gently back to God, and there is another sorrow that only crushes the spirit. Learn to tell them apart. If your grief causes you to trust God's love more deeply, receive it as a gift. But if it persuades you that all your suffering must be punishment, or that you are somehow beyond the reach of mercy, refuse to believe it. I was shown these tender words from our Lord: **Do not blame yourself more than is fitting.** Life itself carries many burdens that are not the fruit of your personal failure. This earthly journey is already a kind of holy discipline, teaching the soul patience, compassion, and dependence upon God. Do not add to its weight by carrying accusations that Love has never spoken.

The Lord does not ask you to invent hardships in order to become holy. He asks you to receive with grace the life that is already before you. Every disappointment, every illness, every hidden struggle, every season of waiting can become a place where you are quietly joined to the love revealed in Christ's Passion. Nothing is wasted when it is entrusted to Him. And even as you carry your cross, do not forget that joy belongs to the remedy. The remedy is not that suffering disappears. The remedy is that Christ walks beside you through it all. The One who will one day be your everlasting joy is already your faithful companion today.

Therefore, whenever your heart grows heavy, flee again to your Lord. Draw near without hesitation. Touch His mercy, and let it cleanse you. Cling to His love, and let it steady your steps. He desires a friendship with you that is wonderfully intimate yet filled with reverence. He is nearer than you dare imagine, yet always worthy of your deepest awe. If you do not yet know how to live in such trust, simply ask Him. He delights to teach willing hearts. For your journey and your destination are held together by one unchanging reality: true love and steadfast trust. Walk in them today, and you are already walking the road that leads home.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Lift me again.*

> **Exhale:** *I trust Your mercy.*
