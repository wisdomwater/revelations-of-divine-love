## Day 37 – The Deepest Desire of Your Soul

> *Beneath every wandering lies a heart that still belongs to God.*

There will be times when you become painfully aware of your own weakness. You will see the ways you fall short of the love you long to live. You may wonder whether your failures have changed how God looks upon you. I was shown that I, too, would stumble, and for a moment a gentle fear entered my heart. Yet before fear could take root, the Lord answered with words more tender than I can fully express: **I keep you safely.** His promise was greater than my weakness. What He spoke to me, He speaks also to every soul that rests in His love.

Do not think that your deepest identity is found in your failures. There is within you a place that sin has never been able to claim. Beneath all your confusion, beneath every selfish desire and every fearful impulse, God has planted a deeper will—a holy longing that has always belonged to Him. This is the truest part of you. Though the surface of your life may become clouded, this hidden place continues to turn toward the light. It is the quiet work of God's own life within your soul, drawing you again and again toward goodness, even after you have wandered.

This is why the Lord never ceases to love you. His love is not measured by your success or diminished by your failures. He sees you not only as you are today, but as He has always known you and as you shall one day be. Even now He beholds you with the same love that will fill heaven itself. What causes your sorrow is not that God has withdrawn His love, but that your own heart so often forgets to rest in it. Much of your struggle comes from living as though you were separated from the One who has never ceased holding you close.

So when you fall, do not remain turned toward your failure. Turn instead toward the Love that is already reaching for you. The deepest movement of your soul is not away from God but toward Him, for He has placed His own desire within you. Trust that holy longing. Nourish it with prayer, kindness, and quiet attention to His presence. Each time you return, you are not becoming someone new so much as awakening to who you have always been in His sight. The God who keeps you will continue His faithful work until every divided part of your heart rests wholly in His everlasting love.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *You keep me safely.*

> **Exhale:** *My heart belongs to You.*
