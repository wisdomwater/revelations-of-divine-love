## Day 42 – Pray with Trust

> *Every prayer is an act of hope rooted in the goodness of God.*

The Lord desires that your prayer be filled not only with longing but also with trust. Too often you ask with sincere words while quietly wondering whether God truly hears you or whether your requests matter. I have known this uncertainty myself. Yet the Lord taught me that prayer begins long before your lips begin to speak. It rises from His own goodness. He awakens your desire, shapes your longing, and gently draws your heart toward His own. When you understand this, prayer is no longer an attempt to persuade a reluctant God. It becomes your joyful participation in a love that has already begun its work within you.

As you pray, allow your will to rest within the will of God. This is not the surrender of hope but its fulfillment. The closer your heart grows to His, the more you begin to desire what Love itself desires. Prayer is not merely asking God to change your circumstances. It is allowing Him to shape your soul until you become more like Christ. In this quiet union, your deepest longing is gradually transformed. What once seemed like separate desires become one shared movement of love between your heart and His.

If you have prayed for something over many months or years and still see no answer, do not lose heart. The Lord showed me that delay is not neglect. Sometimes you are waiting for the right season. Sometimes you are being prepared to receive a greater grace than you first imagined. Sometimes God has something better in store than the gift you thought you needed. His silence is never empty. His timing is never careless. Before you ever prayed, He had already created you in love, redeemed you through Christ, and surrounded your life with countless gifts you did not think to ask for. The God who has already done so much will not fail you in what remains.

So pray with open hands and a steadfast heart. Trust what God is doing even when you cannot yet see it. At the same time, do not become passive, imagining that because God is at work there is no need to pray. The Lord showed me that seeing His work and joining it in prayer belong together. We pray because He is already working, and we rejoice because He has invited us to share in His work. This is the great freedom of prayer: to live each day with a longing for the joy that is coming and a quiet confidence that Love is already bringing it to pass. When your own strength feels small, remember that everything lacking in you is abundantly supplied in Him. There, every fear gives way to trust, and every prayer finds its resting place in the heart of God.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *I trust Your timing.*

> **Exhale:** *My hope rests in You.*
