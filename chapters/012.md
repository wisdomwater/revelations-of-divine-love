## Day 12 – The Love That Never Runs Dry

> *Love gives itself without measure.*

I was shown the wounded body of Christ, marked by suffering beyond anything I could fully express. His skin was torn, and His blood flowed with such abundance that it seemed to cover everything before me. Yet I did not receive this vision as a call to dwell upon pain for its own sake. I was being invited to see the measureless generosity of divine love. Nothing was held back. Love poured itself out completely, freely, and without reserve, so that no one could ever wonder whether God had given enough.

As I beheld this, another understanding quietly unfolded within me. God has filled the earth with water to refresh us, cleanse us, and sustain our lives. Every stream and every rain is a gift of His tender care. Yet these, too, point beyond themselves. They remind us of a deeper cleansing that only divine love can accomplish. There are burdens no outward washing can remove, wounds no earthly remedy can fully heal, and regrets that cling more closely than dust upon the skin. Christ offers something greater. He longs to wash the heart itself, not with condemnation, but with a mercy that reaches where nothing else can.

This love is never scarce. We often imagine grace as though it were limited, wondering whether there is enough forgiveness left for us, enough patience, enough compassion to meet our failures. But what I was shown was abundance beyond measure. The love of Christ overflows every boundary we can imagine. It reaches into the darkest places, lifts what has been bound, seeks what has been lost, and continues to pour itself out wherever hearts are willing to receive it. Divine mercy is not exhausted by our need. It is revealed through it.

So whenever you become aware of your own weakness, do not turn away in shame. Turn instead toward the inexhaustible love that has always been flowing toward you. You need not earn what is already being given. Simply open your heart. The love of Christ has never ceased to move toward the healing of the world, and it has not forgotten you. Even now, it continues its quiet work, cleansing, restoring, and drawing every willing soul more deeply into the endless life of God.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your mercy flows.*
>
> Exhale: *Wash me in love.*
