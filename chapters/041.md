## Day 41 – I Am the Beginning of Your Prayer

> *Every sincere prayer begins in the heart of God before it rises from your own.*

There are times when you pray and feel nothing at all. Your words seem to fall into silence, and your heart remains as dry when you finish as it was when you began. You wonder whether God has heard you, or whether your weakness and unworthiness have become a barrier between you and His love. I have known this weariness myself. Yet the Lord answered my doubts with words that have never ceased to comfort me: **I am the ground of your beseeching.** Before you ever turned your heart toward Him, He had already turned His heart toward you. It was His desire that awakened your desire. It was His love that taught your lips to pray.

Consider what this means. If God Himself has planted the longing within you, why should you fear that He will ignore it? He first wills the gift He longs to give. Then He stirs your heart to desire it. Then He teaches you to ask for it. Finally, He receives your prayer with joy. Your prayer does not persuade God to become loving. It is the evidence that His love is already at work within you. Every true prayer is a quiet partnership between your soul and the Spirit of God, who gently draws your will into harmony with His own.

So do not measure the value of your prayer by your feelings. There are prayers offered with tears and prayers offered with joy, but there are also prayers spoken through exhaustion, distraction, sickness, and emptiness. These are no less precious to God. When your heart feels barren and your words seem weak, continue to pray. When you cannot sense His presence, continue to pray. Even when you believe you cannot pray at all, your simple turning toward Him delights His heart. No sincere prayer is ever wasted. Each one is received with gladness, treasured in His eternal love, and woven into the work He is accomplishing both in you and in the world.

Do not forget that prayer naturally flowers into thanksgiving. As you begin to notice that every longing for God has first arisen from God Himself, gratitude quietly awakens. Sometimes it overflows in joyful praise. At other times it is nothing more than a whispered *thank You* spoken through weary lips. Both are beautiful to Him. Keep turning your heart toward the One who first turned His heart toward you. Seek Him faithfully whether your soul feels full or empty, for the One you are seeking is already nearer than your own breath. One day your every prayer will find its fulfillment, not merely in the gifts you have asked for, but in the Giver Himself. Then you will discover that the truest answer to every prayer has always been Christ, who gives Himself without measure to all who seek Him.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You awaken my prayer.*

> Exhale: *I rest in Your presence.*
