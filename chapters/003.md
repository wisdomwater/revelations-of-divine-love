## Day 3 – Love Stronger Than Death

> *When everything else grows dim, love remains.*

There may come moments when life strips away every illusion of control. The strength you have relied upon falters, your plans dissolve, and even your own body reminds you that it cannot carry you forever. Do not fear such moments more than you must. They are not signs that God has abandoned you. Often they become the places where every lesser certainty falls silent, leaving only the quiet invitation to rest in divine love. When I believed my earthly journey was drawing to its end, I discovered that surrender was gentler than I had imagined.

As I waited between life and death, I searched my heart to see whether there was anything here that held me back. I did not cling to possessions, comfort, or even the fear of suffering. There was only one longing that remained: that I might stay a little longer if it meant I could love God more deeply. Love was the only unfinished work I wished to continue. This taught me something I hope you will also discover. The measure of a life is not how long it lasts, but how fully it has learned to love. Every day becomes precious when it is received as another opportunity to grow into the likeness of Christ.

When my eyes could no longer take in the room around me, they rested upon the face of Christ upon the cross. Darkness gathered at the edges of my vision, yet that holy image remained before me, quietly filled with light. I have found this to be true in more ways than one. There are seasons when fear whispers loudly, when grief overshadows hope, and when the world seems filled with shadows. Even then, keep turning your gaze toward Christ. You need not understand everything that surrounds you. It is enough to behold the One whose love has already entered every darkness before you.

Then, without warning, my pain lifted. I knew this mercy did not mean all questions had been answered, nor that my journey was complete. Instead, it awakened an even deeper desire within me: not simply to escape suffering, but to allow my heart to be joined to the love Christ revealed through His own suffering. I do not ask you to seek pain, for pain alone has no power to heal. I ask only that, whatever joys or sorrows come to you, you allow them to draw you nearer to the heart of God. There you will discover a love that remains steady through life, through death, and through every mystery that lies beyond them.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your love remains.*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in You.*
