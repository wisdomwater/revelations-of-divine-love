## Day 82 – Kept in One Love

> *Neither your falling nor your rising can separate you from the love that keeps you.*

Our gracious Lord let me know that He understands the sincere desire of every soul that loves Him. You long to live faithfully. You would gladly endure hardship if it meant drawing nearer to Him. And if it were possible, you would choose never to wound love by sin again. He knows this longing already. Therefore, do not be crushed by those failures that come against your deepest desire. He does not look upon you with the eyes of an accuser, but with the compassion of One who knows both your weakness and your true intention. This life is not yet the place where every struggle ends. While you remain here, you will still stumble. Yet His love does not grow weary of lifting you up.

When I saw the Lord behold His servant, I noticed something that filled me with peace. He looked with pity, never with contempt. We are often much harsher with ourselves than He is. We become consumed by our shortcomings, while He gently invites us to turn our attention toward His mercy. He does not ask you to pretend that sin does not matter. Rather, He asks you to see it truthfully, to grieve it wisely, and then to let that sorrow lead you back into His embrace. There you discover that He Himself is your healing. As humility grows through the honest knowledge of your weakness, so gratitude grows through the certainty of His unfailing love. These two together become a beautiful offering of praise.

Deep within my soul I heard these words: **I love you, and you love Me, and our love shall never be divided.** They settled every fear that my failures might one day overcome His faithfulness. The love between Christ and the soul is stronger than the frailty that troubles us. He allows many things for our growth, but He never allows His love to be broken. Again He assured me with the same tender promise that has echoed throughout these revelations: **I keep you completely safe.** Hold these words close whenever your heart begins to doubt. They are steadier than your emotions and truer than your fears.

I was not shown that any soul passes through this life without falling. What I was shown is far more comforting: **in both our falling and our rising we are always kept within one Love.** When we look only at ourselves, we quickly lose heart. When we look only at God without remembering our need, we may become careless. Therefore our Lord teaches us two ways of seeing. One is the humble knowledge of our weakness. The other is the joyful vision of His everlasting goodness. Both are true, but they are not equal. The higher vision is always His love. The lower vision is our need. He asks us never to forget our need, yet always to dwell more deeply in His love.

So when you become aware of your weakness, do not remain there. Let it humble you, but do not let it imprison you. Lift your eyes again to the higher truth. You are held by a Love that existed before your first failure and will remain long after your last. Let that Love become the place where your heart lives. There you will find courage to rise again, peace to continue the journey, and hope that will carry you until the day when faith gives way to sight, and you behold forever the face of the One who has kept you in His love all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Keep me in Your love.*

> Exhale: *I rise in Your mercy.*
