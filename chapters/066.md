## Day 66 – Returning to the Light

> *Even when you lose sight of love, love has not lost sight of you.*

The visions I had been given came to their quiet end, and suddenly I found myself once again in the ordinary weight of life. The pain returned to my body. The brightness that had filled my soul seemed to fade like the last light of evening. I felt empty, dry, and unable to hold on to the comfort that had seemed so certain only moments before. In my weakness, I wondered how I could so quickly forget what I had seen. The smallest touch of suffering drew my attention away from the greatness of God's love, and I grieved to discover how frail my own heart could be. If you have known seasons when God once seemed wonderfully near but now feels distant, do not think you are alone. Even after the clearest moments of grace, we remain human, and our weakness often clouds what our hearts once knew with confidence.

For a time I judged myself harshly. I thought my forgetfulness had undone everything. I wondered how I could have believed so deeply while the vision lasted, only to become doubtful again when pain returned. Yet I came to see that even this painful discovery was itself a gift. It revealed not only the weakness of my own strength but the steadfastness of God's. My faithfulness rises and falls like the tide, but His never does. I may lose hold of Him for a moment, but He never loses hold of me. The Lord does not reveal His love because we are strong enough to preserve it. He reveals it because He Himself will preserve us.

In the night I was troubled by fearful visions. Evil seemed to press close, seeking to awaken dread within me. The darkness appeared real enough to overwhelm my heart, and yet it possessed no lasting power. Even when fear came near, it could not truly overcome me. When I awakened, another disturbance followed, and for a moment I believed destruction was at hand. But then I remembered what my Lord had shown me. I turned my heart again toward His promises and toward the faith that had been planted within me. The moment I entrusted myself once more to His love, the fear dissolved like mist before the morning sun. What had seemed so powerful vanished in the presence of Christ.

You will also know such moments. Fear may speak loudly. Shame may tell you that grace has slipped beyond your reach. Old anxieties may return just when you believed they had finally departed. Do not argue endlessly with these voices. Do not give them more attention than they deserve. Instead, turn again toward the truth you have already been shown. Remember the goodness of God. Remember His promises. Remember the love that has carried you before. Faith is often nothing more complicated than returning, again and again, to the One who has never ceased being faithful.

Take courage, then, whenever your heart feels dry or your soul grows weary. Do not measure God's presence by the strength of your feelings. His love is deeper than your emotions, steadier than your thoughts, and stronger than every darkness that seeks to trouble you. Even when your vision grows dim, Christ remains your light. Even when your hands grow weak, His hands still hold you. Return to Him as often as you must. You will always find Him waiting with the same quiet mercy, ready to restore your peace and remind you that nothing—not fear, not suffering, not even your own forgetfulness—can separate you from the love that has claimed you.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are my light.*

> Exhale: *I return to You.*
