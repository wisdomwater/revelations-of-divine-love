## Day 59 – Christ, Our True Mother

> *The love that gave you life is the same love that patiently raises you whenever you fall.*

I was shown that one of the greatest mysteries of God's love is this: He does not merely overcome evil by His power, but transforms it through His goodness. Wickedness is never stronger than love. Though it is permitted for a time, it cannot have the final word. Mercy rises to meet it. Grace answers it. And in ways beyond our understanding, God gathers even the wounds of our lives into His healing purpose. This does not make evil good, nor does it lessen the sorrow it brings. Rather, it reveals that divine love is so abundant that nothing can finally defeat its desire to restore.

Because of this, I came to know Christ as our true Mother. Just as a mother carries, nourishes, protects, and patiently forms the life of her child, so Christ does for every soul. Our first life springs from His creative love, and our new life grows through His mercy. He does not grow weary of caring for us when we are weak. He does not withdraw when we stumble. His love surrounds us with a tenderness that never ceases. Every act of forgiveness, every quiet strengthening, every gentle invitation to begin again flows from this mothering heart of Christ.

Then I heard the Lord speak words that gathered every longing of the soul into Himself: **I am.** *I am the strength of the Father. I am the wisdom of the Mother. I am the light and the grace that is all blessed love. I am the One who awakens your love. I am the One who stirs your longing. I am the endless fulfillment of every true desire.* As these words settled within me, I understood that every holy longing already begins in God. The desire you have for Him is itself His gift. Long before you reach toward Him, His love has already reached toward you.

The soul becomes most like God not by exalting itself, but by becoming gentle. I saw that the highest place is found through humility, the strongest life through meekness, and the deepest wisdom through love. These are not signs of weakness but of true strength. From this hidden ground, God grows patience, compassion, courage, and holiness within us. As mercy softens the heart, it also teaches us to hate only what destroys love. We learn to resist sin not because we are driven by fear, but because we have come to cherish the goodness that God is creating within us.

I was shown three beautiful ways that Christ mothers every soul. First, He gives us life by creating us in love. Then He takes our humanity upon Himself, entering our weakness so that He may restore us from within. Finally, He continues His mothering work every day, pouring His grace through every corner of our lives without measure or end. This loving care stretches farther than we can imagine—wider than the world, deeper than our sorrow, higher than our hope. Yet it is all one love, endlessly flowing from the heart of God.

Rest today beneath that gentle care. You do not need to earn the love that nurses your weary soul. You do not need to prove yourself worthy of the One who has already claimed you as His own. Let yourself be held. Let yourself be taught. Let yourself be healed. The Christ who gave you life continues to carry you with infinite tenderness, and His patient love will not cease until every wound has become a doorway into joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Mothering Christ, hold me.*

> **Exhale:** *Grow Your life in me.*
