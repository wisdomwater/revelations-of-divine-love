## Day 51 – The Servant Who Fell with Us

> *Christ does not look upon your fall from far away; He has entered it, borne it, and raised it into glory.*

I was shown a wonder that I could not quickly understand. I saw a lord sitting in peace and rest, and before him stood a servant, ready and eager to do his will. The lord looked upon the servant with deep tenderness and sent him forth. And the servant, moved by love, did not walk slowly or carefully, but ran with all haste to fulfill the desire of his lord. Yet as he ran, he fell into a deep place and was badly hurt. He groaned and struggled, but could not rise. His pain was great, but the greatest pain was this: he could not turn his face to see the loving lord who remained so near.

As I looked upon him, I searched to see whether there was blame in him. I wondered whether the lord would accuse him for his falling. But I saw no blame at all. His will had been good. His love had been whole. He had fallen not through laziness or rebellion, but through the very haste of love. Though he lay bruised, weakened, blinded, and alone, his lord gazed upon him with pity and joy together. Pity, because the servant suffered so greatly. Joy, because the lord already saw the restoration that would come. The fall was real, the pain was real, the helplessness was real; yet none of these was the final truth.

Then I began to understand that this servant was shown to me as Adam, and in Adam all humanity. For before God, all humanity is gathered into one. Yet the servant was also Christ, the Son of God, who would not be separated from Adam in his fall. When humanity fell into weakness, confusion, sorrow, and death, the Son fell with us—not into sin, but into our condition. He descended into the womb of Mary, into our flesh, into our poverty, into our labor, into our pain. He took upon Himself the worn garment of Adam so that He might lift Adam back into glory.

This is the marvel: our Father does not look upon us apart from His beloved Son. Christ is our Head, and we are His members. He has joined Himself to us so deeply that our wounds became His wounds, our burden became His burden, and our restoration became His joy. Therefore the Father does not assign blame to us as though Christ had not come. He sees us in His Son, and He sees His Son in us. This does not make sin small. It makes mercy immeasurably great. It shows that God’s answer to our falling is not abandonment, but union.

The servant’s torn garment was made fair, bright, and new. The one who lay struggling rose in glory. The laborer who stood in poverty now sits in rest and peace. And I was shown that we are His crown—His joy before the Father, the delight of heaven, the fruit of His travail. Carry this gently into your own places of falling. When you cannot rise by your own strength, when you cannot even turn your face toward comfort, Christ is not far from you. He is nearer than your own breath, bearing you in Himself, and what He has taken into His love He will not leave unrestored.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Christ is with me.*

> Exhale: *I am raised in Him.*
