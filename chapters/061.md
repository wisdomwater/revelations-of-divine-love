## Day 61 – Held Through Every Fall

> *No failure is strong enough to break the love that has already claimed you.*

There are times when you will wonder if all the grace you have received has somehow been lost. You will stumble in ways you thought were behind you. Old fears may return. Familiar weaknesses may rise again, and you may be tempted to believe that all your growth has come to nothing. Do not believe this. I have been shown that our falls do not surprise God, nor do they erase the work His love has begun within us. Again and again He awakens our understanding, steadies our steps, quiets our troubled conscience, and lifts the weight from our hearts. And whenever we fall, He is already drawing near, not with condemnation, but with a gentle touch that invites us to stand once more. His purpose has never been to shame you into faithfulness, but to love you into wholeness.

You may ask why such falling is permitted at all. I wondered the same. Yet I was shown that there is a wisdom hidden even here. If we never saw our own frailty, we might quietly begin to trust ourselves more than the God who carries us. We would know little of our need, and even less of the greatness of divine mercy. But when we see both our weakness and His unwavering compassion, something beautiful is born within us. Humility takes root—not the humiliation that despairs of itself, but the quiet freedom of knowing that everything good has always been a gift. One day you will see clearly that, though you sinned in many ways, you were never abandoned by divine love. Your worth was never diminished in God's sight. The very places where you feared love had failed will become places where you marvel most deeply at how faithful love has always been.

Think of a little child who falls while learning to walk. A loving mother does not delight in the fall, nor does she abandon the child because of it. She gathers the child close, comforts the tears, and patiently teaches those unsteady feet to walk again. So it is with Christ, your true Mother. When shame tells you to hide, love invites you to come closer. When fear whispers that you have made yourself unworthy, He calls you to run toward Him with the honesty of a child. Bring Him your failure without disguise. Tell Him where you are wounded. Tell Him where you cannot mend yourself. He already knows, and His mercy has never depended upon your ability to heal yourself. If comfort does not come immediately, do not mistake His silence for absence. A wise mother sometimes allows her child to cry for a little while, not because love has withdrawn, but because deeper healing is quietly taking place beneath the tears.

Remember also that you were never meant to walk this journey alone. When your own faith feels fragile, let yourself be carried by the greater faith of Christ and by the fellowship of all who belong to Him. There are days when one heart feels broken beyond repair, yet the living body of Christ remains whole, holding each weary soul within its embrace. Drink deeply from the mercy that continually flows from His life. His wounds remain open to heal yours. His hands are never too weary to lift you. His whole delight is to care for those He loves.

So whenever you find yourself lying in the dust of disappointment, do not linger there in despair. Lift your eyes toward the One who has never ceased watching over you. Hear Him speak these gentle words into the deepest places of your heart: **I keep you completely safe.** Trust them more than your failures. Trust them more than your fears. For the love that first called you has never loosened its hold, and it never will.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *You hold me fast.*

> **Exhale:** *Your love never fails.*
