## Day 71 – The Face Turned Toward You

> *The face of Christ is always turned toward you with love.*

I have come to know that the face of our Lord is never turned away from you. He looks upon you always, not with impatience, disappointment, or indifference, but with a love that longs for your joy. His gaze is steady because His love is steady. Even when you cannot lift your eyes toward Him, His eyes remain upon you. He delights whenever your heart turns toward Him, not because He requires your affection to complete Himself, but because love always rejoices when it is returned. The happiness of God is not distant from your own. He desires to draw your outward life into the quiet joy that already lives within Him, until your heart and His beat together in one unbroken communion.

As I beheld Him, I was shown three ways in which His countenance is revealed to us. The first is the face of His Passion. It is the face that bore suffering for love's sake. It is marked by sacrifice, yet never by despair. Whenever you carry grief, loss, or pain, Christ does not watch from a safe distance. He meets you with the face of One who has suffered too. His wounds become a place where your own wounds are understood. Because He entered sorrow willingly, no sorrow you bear is foreign to Him.

The second is the face of compassion. This is the countenance He turns toward you whenever you become aware of your weakness or your sin. His first movement is not accusation but mercy. He knows how easily you become discouraged by your failures, and so He watches over you with great tenderness, protecting you even when you scarcely know how to protect yourself. His pity is never condescending. It is the love of One who understands your frailty completely and refuses to let it have the final word. When you entrust yourself to His mercy, you are held more securely than you can imagine.

Yet there is another face that He allows us to glimpse from time to time: His face of everlasting joy. Sometimes, without warning, a quiet gladness rises within the soul. A burden feels lighter. Prayer becomes sweet. Beauty awakens wonder. Peace settles where anxiety once lived. These moments are gifts of grace, small openings through which the light of eternity shines into the present. They do not remove every hardship, but they remind you of where your life is ultimately leading. Heaven is not merely a place you hope to reach someday. Its joy already begins to touch you whenever Christ gently lifts your heart into His own.

Receive all three of these faces as gifts of divine love. In suffering, look upon the face of His Passion and know that you are not alone. In failure, look upon the face of His compassion and know that mercy is already reaching toward you. In moments of quiet joy, look upon His radiant countenance and give thanks for the home that awaits you. Through them all, the same Lord is looking upon you with unchanging affection. His face does not change because His love does not change. And as you continue to behold Him, little by little your own face will begin to reflect His peace, until one day you will see Him as He is and rejoice with Him forever.

**Breath Prayer**

> **Inhale:** *Your face shines upon me.*

> **Exhale:** *I rest in Your love.*
